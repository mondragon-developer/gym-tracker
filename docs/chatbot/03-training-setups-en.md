# Gym Tracker — Warm-Ups & Weekly Training Setups (English)

This document gives the chatbot ready-made answers for "how should I warm up?" and "how do I organize my week if I can train 3 / 4 / 5 / 6 days?". Every exercise named here exists in the Gym Tracker app — search it by its English name in "Add Exercise". These are **general templates**, not personalized programs, and none of this is medical advice: users with pain, injuries, or health conditions should consult a doctor or certified professional before following any plan.

Two app facts that matter for setup:
- A day's muscle-group label (✏️ button, up to 3 groups, or "Rest") is **just a label** — you can add any exercise to any day. The Add Exercise picker pre-filters to the day's group, but the **All** tab shows every exercise.
- Strength exercises log sets/reps/weight; Cardio and Combat exercises log minutes (1–120). Timed holds like Plank are strength-type: log the hold seconds in the Reps field (e.g. "30-60s").

## Warming up (before every session)

**1. General warm-up — 5–10 minutes of light cardio.** Trackable in the app: add one of these as a Cardio exercise with a 5–10 minute duration: Jump Rope, Jumping Jacks, High Knees, Treadmill Running, Stationary Bike, Elliptical, or Rowing Machine. Easy pace — you should be able to hold a conversation.

**2. Dynamic preparation — 2–3 minutes (off-app).** Arm circles, shoulder rolls, hip circles, leg swings, and 10–15 slow bodyweight squats or easy push-ups. Move the joints you're about to train through their full range.

**3. Warm-up sets for your first strength exercise.** Before your working weight on the first big lift of the day (squat, bench, deadlift, press or row):
- Very light / empty bar × 10 reps
- ~50% of working weight × 5 reps
- ~70% of working weight × 3 reps
Then start your logged working sets. Later exercises for the same muscles usually need only one light set or none.

**Cooling down.** 3–5 minutes of easy cardio (slow walk or easy bike) and calm breathing. Gentle stretching afterward is optional and done off-app — the app has no stretching exercises.

## Choosing your weekly frequency

- **3 days** — full body each session. Best for beginners and busy schedules; most results per hour invested.
- **4 days** — upper/lower split. Great balance of volume and recovery.
- **5 days** — one muscle-group focus per day ("bro split") or upper/lower + push/pull/legs hybrid.
- **6 days** — Push/Pull/Legs twice per week. This is the app's pre-loaded default plan.
- Always keep **at least one full rest day** per week. If in doubt, pick fewer days — the plan you actually complete beats the perfect one you skip. The weekly progress bar and 📊 Weekly Summary help you see what you really finished.

## 3-day full body (Monday / Wednesday / Friday)

Set Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday (and Sunday) to **Rest** with the ✏️ button. Give each training day a label like Chest + Back + Legs. Rest 2–3 minutes on the big lifts, 1–2 on the rest.

**Day A (Monday):** Barbell Squats 3×8-10 · Barbell Bench Press 3×8-10 · Seated Cable Rows 3×10-12 · Overhead Press 2×10-12 · Plank 3× (hold 30–60s)

**Day B (Wednesday):** Deadlifts 3×6-8 · Incline Dumbbell Press 3×10-12 · Lat Pulldowns 3×10-12 · Lunges 3×12-15 · Leg Raises 3×12-15

**Day C (Friday):** Leg Press 3×12-15 · Dumbbell Press 3×8-12 · Bent-Over Barbell Rows 3×8-10 · Lateral Raises 3×12-15 · Barbell Curls 2×10-12 · Rope Pushdowns 2×10-12

## 4-day upper/lower (Monday / Tuesday / Thursday / Friday)

Wednesday, Saturday, Sunday → Rest (or light cardio, see below).

**Upper A (Monday):** Barbell Bench Press 4×6-10 · Bent-Over Barbell Rows 4×8-10 · Overhead Press 3×8-10 · Lat Pulldowns 3×10-12 · Barbell Curls 2×10-12 · Rope Pushdowns 2×10-12

**Lower A (Tuesday):** Barbell Squats 4×6-10 · Romanian Deadlifts 3×8-12 · Leg Press 3×12-15 · Standing Calf Raises 4×15-20 · Plank 3× (30–60s)

**Upper B (Thursday):** Incline Dumbbell Press 4×8-12 · Pull-ups 3×6-10 (or Lat Pulldowns) · Arnold Press 3×10-12 · Seated Cable Rows 3×10-12 · Hammer Curls 2×12-15 · Skull Crushers 2×10-12

**Lower B (Friday):** Deadlifts 3×5-8 · Front Squats 3×8-10 · Lying Leg Curls 3×12-15 · Seated Calf Raises 3×15-20 · Hanging Leg Raises 3×10-15

## 5-day muscle-group split (Monday–Friday)

One focus per day; weekend rest. Label each day with its group.

**Monday — Chest:** Barbell Bench Press 4×6-10 · Incline Dumbbell Press 3×8-12 · Cable Flyes 3×12-15 · Push-Ups 2× to a comfortable limit

**Tuesday — Back:** Deadlifts 3×5-8 · Pull-ups 3×6-10 · Seated Cable Rows 3×10-12 · Face Pulls 3×15-20 · Barbell Shrugs 2×12-15

**Wednesday — Shoulders + Abs:** Military Press 4×6-10 · Lateral Raises 4×12-15 · Reverse Pec Deck Flyes 3×12-15 · Cable Crunches 3×12-15 · Plank 2× (30–60s)

**Thursday — Legs:** Barbell Squats 4×6-10 · Romanian Deadlifts 3×8-12 · Leg Extensions 3×12-15 · Lying Leg Curls 3×12-15 · Standing Calf Raises 4×15-20

**Friday — Arms + Forearms:** Close-Grip Bench Press 4×8-12 · Barbell Curls 4×8-12 · Rope Pushdowns 3×10-15 · Hammer Curls 3×10-15 · Wrist Curls 2×15-20 · Dead Hangs 2× (max comfortable hold)

## 6-day Push/Pull/Legs — the app's default

The app ships with this exact plan already loaded (Push Mon/Thu, Pull Tue/Fri, Legs Wed/Sat, Sunday rest — full listing in the App Guide). Nothing to configure: it's there on first launch. To return to it after customizing, use **Reset Day** on a single day or **Reset Week** — warning: reset discards your customizations and restores the default plan.

## Home / bodyweight week (no gym)

All these exist in the app and need little or no equipment. Example 3-day pattern:

**Day A:** Push-Ups 3× to a comfortable limit · Inverted Rows 3×8-12 (sturdy table or low bar) · Glute Bridges 3×15-20 · Wall Sits 3× (30–60s) · Plank 3× (30–60s)

**Day B:** Jumping Jacks 5 min · Walking Lunges 3×12-15 · Diamond Push-Ups 3× to limit · Bird Dog 3×10 per side · Bicycle Crunches 3×15-20 · Mountain Climbers 3×30s

**Day C:** Burpees 3×10-15 · Step-Ups 3×12-15 · Bench Dips 3×10-15 (sturdy chair) · Dead Bug 3×10 per side · Side Plank 2× per side (20–40s) · Jump Rope 10 min

If you have a pull-up bar, add Pull-ups or Dead Hangs on any day.

## Adding cardio to any plan

2–3 sessions per week is a solid general target. Two flavors:
- **Steady state, 20–40 min:** Treadmill Running, Cycling, Stationary Bike, Elliptical, Swimming, Rowing Machine, Stair Climber — a pace where talking is possible.
- **Intervals, 10–20 min:** Sprint Intervals, Assault Bike, Battle Ropes, Jump Rope HIIT — short hard efforts with easy recovery in between.

In the app: add **Cardio** as a day's second or third muscle-group label, or put cardio on rest days. Doing cardio after weights (or at another time of day) is a common default so it doesn't steal energy from lifting.

## Optional boxing/conditioning day

A fun Combat-category session, all logged in minutes: Jump Rope 10 min → Shadowboxing 3 rounds of 3 min (1 min rest) → Heavy Bag Rounds 4×3 min → Boxing Footwork Drills 10 min → Plank 3× (30–60s). Label the day Combat + Cardio + Abs.

## Volume, progression, and rest — rules of thumb (non-medical)

- **Weekly volume:** roughly 10–20 hard sets per muscle per week works well for most people. The 📊 Weekly Summary shows **Sets per muscle group** — the easy way to check.
- **Progression:** when you hit the top of the rep range on all sets with good form, add a little weight the following week. **Start New Week** carries your exercises and weights forward, so progression is just editing the number up.
- **Rest between sets:** ~2–3 minutes on heavy compound lifts, ~1–2 minutes on isolation work.
- **Easier weeks:** every 6–10 weeks, or when performance drops and everything feels heavy, taking a lighter week (less weight or fewer sets) is a common, sensible practice.
- **Sleep and recovery matter** — generic advice: muscles grow between sessions, not during them.

## Setting any plan up in the app — quick steps

1. Expand a day → tap **✏️** → select up to 3 muscle-group labels (or **Rest**) → **Done**.
2. Tap **Add Exercise** → search the English name → set Target Sets/Reps (or minutes for Cardio/Combat) → **Add to Workout**. Use the **All** tab if the exercise belongs to a different group than the day's label.
3. Drag to reorder; delete what you don't want.
4. Train, mark ✓ done or ✗ skipped, and check the 📊 Weekly Summary.
5. Tap **🔄 Start New Week** each week — your plan and weights carry forward automatically.
