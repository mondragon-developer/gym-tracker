# Gym Tracker — Exercise Guide (English)

This guide covers all 159 exercises available in the Gym Tracker app (gymworkoutjm.vercel.app). Strength exercises log **Sets, Reps, and Weight (lbs)**. **Cardio** and **Combat** exercises are time-based and log **Duration in minutes (1–120)**. Most gym exercises (122 of 159) include a visual demo: tap the ▶ button on the exercise to open "How to do this exercise", which shows the full range of motion from start to finish. Cardio and Combat exercises have no demo images. To find any exercise, go to **"Add Exercise"** and use the search bar or the muscle-group filter.

**Safety disclaimer:** This guide gives general technique pointers only — it is NOT medical advice. Always use weights you can control through a full range of motion, warm up before training, and stop if you feel sharp pain. Consult a qualified professional (doctor or certified trainer) for pain, injuries, or any health condition.

## Chest

### Barbell Bench Press (Press de Banca con Barra)
- **Muscles:** Chest (pectoralis major); front delts and triceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, flat bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on the bench with eyes under the bar, feet flat on the floor.
  2. Grip slightly wider than shoulder width, unrack, and hold the bar over your chest.
  3. Lower the bar under control to your mid-chest, elbows about 45–70° from your torso.
  4. Press back up to full arm extension without locking elbows harshly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bouncing the bar off the chest.
  - Flaring elbows straight out to the sides.
  - Lifting hips off the bench.
- **Tip:** Inhale on the way down, exhale as you press; 5–10 reps works well for strength and muscle.

### Incline Dumbbell Press (Press Inclinado con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Upper chest; front delts and triceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells, incline bench (30–45°).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit back on an incline bench with a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder level.
  2. Press the dumbbells up and slightly together until arms are extended.
  3. Lower slowly until you feel a stretch across the upper chest.
  4. Repeat without clanging the dumbbells at the top.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Setting the bench too steep, turning it into a shoulder press.
  - Arching the lower back excessively.
- **Tip:** 8–12 reps with a 2–3 second lowering phase builds the upper chest effectively.

### Cable Flyes (Aperturas en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Chest; front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with two pulleys.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set pulleys at chest height, grab a handle in each hand, and step forward into a staggered stance.
  2. Start with arms open wide, slight bend in the elbows.
  3. Bring the handles together in front of your chest in a hugging arc.
  4. Return slowly to the stretched position.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bending the elbows more as you pull (turning it into a press).
  - Using momentum from the torso.
- **Tip:** Keep constant tension — 10–15 controlled reps and squeeze the chest for a second at the middle.

### Dumbbell Press (Press con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Chest; front delts and triceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells, flat bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie flat with a dumbbell in each hand at chest level, palms facing forward.
  2. Press both dumbbells up until arms are extended over your chest.
  3. Lower under control until elbows drop slightly below the bench line.
  4. Press back up along the same path.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lowering too fast and losing control at the bottom.
  - Letting the dumbbells drift toward your face.
- **Tip:** Dumbbells demand more stability than a barbell — start lighter than your bench press and work in the 8–12 rep range.

### Push-Ups (Flexiones)
- **Muscles:** Chest, triceps, front delts; core stabilizes.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight only.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set hands slightly wider than shoulders, body in a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Lower your chest to just above the floor, elbows about 45° from your torso.
  3. Push back up to full arm extension.
  4. Keep your core braced the whole set.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Sagging hips or piking the butt up.
  - Cutting the range short.
- **Tip:** Too hard? Elevate your hands on a bench. Too easy? Elevate your feet or add a slow 3-second descent.

### Machine Chest Press (Press de Pecho en Máquina)
- **Muscles:** Chest; front delts and triceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Chest press machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Adjust the seat so the handles line up with your mid-chest.
  2. Grip the handles, keep your back against the pad.
  3. Press forward until arms are extended without locking out hard.
  4. Return slowly until you feel a chest stretch.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Seat set too high or low, shifting stress to the shoulders.
  - Letting the weight stack slam down between reps.
- **Tip:** Great beginner option — the fixed path lets you focus on squeezing the chest for 10–15 reps.

### Dumbbell Flyes (Aperturas con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Chest; front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells, flat bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie flat holding dumbbells above your chest, palms facing each other.
  2. With a slight, fixed elbow bend, open your arms in a wide arc.
  3. Lower until you feel a comfortable stretch across the chest.
  4. Bring the dumbbells back together over your chest in the same arc.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Going too heavy and turning the fly into a press.
  - Dropping the arms too deep and straining the shoulders.
- **Tip:** Use light weight, 10–15 slow reps — this is a stretch-and-squeeze movement, not a strength lift.

### Cable Crossovers (Cruces en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Chest (lower/inner emphasis with high pulleys); front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with two high pulleys.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set both pulleys high, grab the handles, and step forward with a slight lean.
  2. Start with arms wide, elbows slightly bent.
  3. Pull the handles down and across until your hands meet (or cross) in front of your hips.
  4. Return slowly to the stretched start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hunching the shoulders forward.
  - Rushing the return and losing tension.
- **Tip:** Squeeze the chest hard for one second when the handles meet; 12–15 reps is a good target.

### Pec Deck Machine (Máquina Peck Deck)
- **Muscles:** Chest; front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Pec deck (butterfly) machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Adjust the seat so the handles or pads sit at chest height.
  2. Sit with your back flat against the pad and grip the handles with a slight elbow bend.
  3. Bring the handles together in front of your chest.
  4. Open back up slowly until you feel a chest stretch.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Opening too far back and straining the shoulders.
  - Using momentum instead of a controlled squeeze.
- **Tip:** Ideal isolation finisher — 12–15 reps with a 1-second squeeze at the middle.

### Floor Press (Press de Suelo)
- **Muscles:** Chest and triceps; front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells, floor.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on the floor (under a rack if using a barbell), knees bent, feet flat.
  2. Hold the weight over your chest with arms extended.
  3. Lower until your upper arms (triceps) touch the floor — pause briefly.
  4. Press back up to full extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bouncing the elbows off the floor.
  - Lifting the hips to cheat the press.
- **Tip:** The shortened range is shoulder-friendly and great for triceps lockout strength — try 6–10 reps.

### Decline Bench Press (Press Declinado)
- **Muscles:** Lower chest; triceps and front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, decline bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on a decline bench with legs secured under the pads.
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulders and unrack.
  3. Lower the bar under control to your lower chest.
  4. Press back up until your arms are extended.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lowering the bar toward the neck instead of the lower chest.
  - Bouncing out of the bottom position.
- **Tip:** Use a spotter or safety pins when going heavy on a decline; 6–10 reps works well.

### Diamond Push-Ups (Flexiones Diamante)
- **Muscles:** Triceps and inner chest; front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight only.
- **How to do it:**
  1. From a push-up position, place your hands together under your chest, thumbs and index fingers forming a diamond.
  2. Keep your body in a straight line and lower your chest toward your hands.
  3. Press back up to full extension, keeping elbows tracking back, not flared.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Flaring elbows wide and stressing the wrists.
  - Sagging hips.
- **Tip:** Harder than standard push-ups — do them from your knees or with hands elevated until you can manage 8–10 clean reps.

### Chest Dips (Fondos para Pecho)
- **Muscles:** Lower chest and triceps; front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Parallel bars / dip station (bodyweight, optionally weighted).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Support yourself on parallel bars with arms locked.
  2. Lean your torso forward and let your elbows travel back as you lower.
  3. Descend until your shoulders are about level with your elbows.
  4. Press back up while keeping the forward lean to emphasize the chest.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Staying fully upright (that shifts work to triceps).
  - Dropping too deep and straining the shoulders.
- **Tip:** Master 8–12 bodyweight reps before adding weight with a dip belt.

### Landmine Press (Press con Mina Terrestre)
- **Muscles:** Upper chest and front delts; triceps and core assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell anchored in a landmine attachment (or a corner).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand (or half-kneel) holding the free end of the barbell at your shoulder with one hand.
  2. Brace your core and press the bar up and forward along its natural arc.
  3. Extend the arm fully, then lower back to the shoulder under control.
  4. Complete all reps, then switch sides.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning back and arching the lower back.
  - Pressing with a shrugged, tense shoulder.
- **Tip:** Very shoulder-friendly pressing angle — 8–12 reps per side; the half-kneeling version adds core work.

## Back

### Pull-ups (Dominadas)
- **Muscles:** Lats and upper back; biceps and forearms assist.
- **Equipment:** Pull-up bar (bodyweight, optionally weighted).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hang from the bar with an overhand grip slightly wider than shoulders.
  2. Pull your chest toward the bar by driving your elbows down.
  3. Get your chin over the bar, pause briefly.
  4. Lower under control to a full hang.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Kipping or swinging for momentum.
  - Stopping halfway up or halfway down.
- **Tip:** Can't do one yet? Use band assistance or slow negatives (jump up, lower for 5 seconds); aim to build to 5–10 strict reps.

### Seated Cable Rows (Remo Sentado en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Mid-back (rhomboids, lats, traps); biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable row station with a close-grip handle.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with feet on the platform, knees slightly bent, and grab the handle.
  2. Sit tall with a neutral spine, arms extended.
  3. Pull the handle to your lower ribs, driving elbows back and squeezing the shoulder blades.
  4. Return slowly to full arm extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rocking the torso back and forth.
  - Shrugging the shoulders toward the ears.
- **Tip:** Think "elbows to back pockets" — 10–12 controlled reps beats heavy swinging every time.

### Lat Pulldowns (Jalón al Pecho)
- **Muscles:** Lats; biceps, rear delts, and mid-back assist.
- **Equipment:** Lat pulldown machine (cable).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with thighs secured under the pads and grip the bar wider than shoulders.
  2. Lean back slightly and lift your chest.
  3. Pull the bar down to your upper chest, driving elbows down and back.
  4. Let the bar rise slowly to full arm extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pulling the bar behind the neck.
  - Leaning way back and heaving with body weight.
- **Tip:** Great pull-up substitute — 10–15 reps focusing on feeling the lats, not the arms.

### Bent-Over Barbell Rows (Remo con Barra Inclinado)
- **Muscles:** Lats, mid-back, rear delts; biceps, lower back, and hamstrings assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hinge at the hips until your torso is around 45° (or lower), knees slightly bent, back flat.
  2. Let the bar hang at arm's length below your shoulders.
  3. Row the bar to your lower ribs/upper abdomen.
  4. Lower under control without letting your torso rise.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rounding the lower back.
  - Standing up more each rep to move heavier weight.
- **Tip:** Keep your torso angle fixed for the whole set — 6–10 quality reps with moderate weight.

### Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows (Remo con Mancuerna a Un Brazo)
- **Muscles:** Lats and mid-back; biceps and rear delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell, bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Place one knee and the same-side hand on a bench; keep your back flat and parallel to the floor.
  2. Hold the dumbbell in the free hand, arm hanging straight down.
  3. Row the dumbbell to your hip, driving the elbow up and back.
  4. Lower slowly; finish the set, then switch sides.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Twisting the torso to hoist the weight.
  - Rowing toward the shoulder instead of the hip.
- **Tip:** Allow a slight stretch at the bottom of each rep; 8–12 reps per arm.

### T-Bar Rows (Remo en T)
- **Muscles:** Mid-back, lats, rear delts; biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** T-bar row machine or landmine barbell with a close-grip handle.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Straddle the bar, hinge at the hips with a flat back, and grip the handles.
  2. Deadlift the weight to the start position, arms extended.
  3. Row the handle to your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  4. Lower under control without dropping your chest.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Standing too upright and turning it into a shrug.
  - Jerking the weight with the hips.
- **Tip:** The close neutral grip lets you go relatively heavy — 8–10 controlled reps.

### Face Pulls (Jalón a la Cara)
- **Muscles:** Rear delts, traps, and rotator cuff muscles.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with rope attachment, set at upper-chest/face height.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Grab the rope ends with palms facing in and step back until the cable is taut.
  2. Pull the rope toward your face, splitting the ends past your ears.
  3. Finish with elbows high and hands beside your head, squeezing the rear delts.
  4. Return slowly to the start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Using too much weight and turning it into a row.
  - Letting the elbows drop low.
- **Tip:** Superb for posture and shoulder health — high reps (15–20) with light weight, done often.

### Straight-Arm Pulldowns (Pullover en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Lats; triceps (long head) and core assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a high pulley and straight bar or rope.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand facing the cable, grab the bar with straight arms at shoulder height, and hinge slightly forward.
  2. Keeping elbows almost locked, pull the bar down in an arc to your thighs.
  3. Squeeze the lats at the bottom.
  4. Let the bar rise slowly back to shoulder height.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bending the elbows and turning it into a pushdown.
  - Rounding the back at the bottom.
- **Tip:** Perfect for learning to "feel" the lats before rows or pulldowns — 12–15 light reps.

### Barbell Shrugs (Encogimientos con Barra)
- **Muscles:** Upper trapezius; forearms grip.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding a barbell in front of your thighs, arms straight, shoulder-width grip.
  2. Shrug your shoulders straight up toward your ears as high as possible.
  3. Pause one second at the top.
  4. Lower slowly to a full stretch.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rolling the shoulders in circles.
  - Bending the elbows to lift the bar higher.
- **Tip:** Straps let you hold heavier weight than your grip allows; 10–15 reps with a hard 1-second squeeze.

### Dumbbell Shrugs (Encogimientos con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Upper trapezius; forearms grip.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand tall with a dumbbell in each hand at your sides, palms facing your body.
  2. Shrug both shoulders straight up as high as you can.
  3. Hold the top position briefly.
  4. Lower slowly and let the traps stretch at the bottom.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Using momentum from the knees.
  - Tucking the chin or jutting the head forward.
- **Tip:** Dumbbells allow a more natural arm path than a barbell — 10–15 slow reps.

### Farmer's Walk (Paseo del Granjero)
- **Muscles:** Traps, forearms/grip, core; whole body works isometrically.
- **Equipment:** Heavy dumbbells, kettlebells, or farmer's-walk handles.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Deadlift a heavy weight in each hand with a flat back.
  2. Stand tall — chest up, shoulders back, core braced.
  3. Walk with short, quick, controlled steps for a set distance or time.
  4. Set the weights down with a flat back.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning forward or letting the shoulders round.
  - Taking long, unstable strides.
- **Tip:** Log it as sets — e.g., 3–4 carries of 20–40 steps. This is essentially the same movement as **Farmer's Carry** (listed under Forearms).

### Rack Pulls (Peso Muerto Parcial)
- **Muscles:** Upper back, traps, lower back, glutes; grip works hard.
- **Equipment:** Barbell set on rack pins or blocks (about knee height).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set the bar at or just below knee height in a power rack.
  2. Hinge at the hips, grip the bar shoulder-width, flatten your back.
  3. Drive through your legs and hips to stand tall, locking out with squeezed glutes.
  4. Lower the bar back to the pins under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rounding the back off the pins.
  - Hyperextending (leaning back) at lockout.
- **Tip:** A partial-range deadlift that lets you overload the upper back — 3–6 heavy, crisp reps.

### Cable Shrugs (Encogimientos en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Upper trapezius; forearms grip.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with low pulley and straight bar or two handles.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand facing the low pulley, grab the attachment with straight arms.
  2. Stand tall with the cable taut.
  3. Shrug your shoulders straight up, pause, and squeeze.
  4. Lower slowly against the constant cable tension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning back to lift the weight.
  - Cutting the stretch short at the bottom.
- **Tip:** The cable keeps tension through the whole range — great for 12–15 controlled reps.

### Trap Bar Shrugs (Encogimientos con Barra Trap)
- **Muscles:** Upper trapezius; forearms and core assist.
- **Equipment:** Trap (hex) bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand inside the trap bar and deadlift it to standing with neutral grips at your sides.
  2. Keeping arms straight, shrug your shoulders as high as possible.
  3. Pause at the top.
  4. Lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bending the elbows.
  - Bouncing reps with knee drive.
- **Tip:** The neutral grip and centered load let you go heavier than dumbbell shrugs — 8–12 reps.

### Chin-ups (Dominadas Supinas)
- **Muscles:** Lats and biceps; forearms and mid-back assist.
- **Equipment:** Pull-up bar (bodyweight, optionally weighted).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hang from the bar with an underhand (palms facing you) grip, hands about shoulder width.
  2. Pull your chest to the bar, leading with your elbows.
  3. Get your chin clearly over the bar.
  4. Lower slowly to a dead hang.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Half reps that never reach a full hang.
  - Swinging the legs for momentum.
- **Tip:** Easier than pull-ups for most people thanks to extra biceps involvement — a great first milestone before overhand pull-ups.

### Pendlay Rows (Remo Pendlay)
- **Muscles:** Mid-back and lats; lower back, glutes, and biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell (bar starts on the floor each rep).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set up like a bent-over row but with your torso parallel to the floor, bar on the ground.
  2. Grip the bar, brace, and row it explosively to your lower chest.
  3. Squeeze, then return the bar completely to the floor.
  4. Reset your position before each rep.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Raising the torso during the pull.
  - Bouncing the bar instead of resetting each rep.
- **Tip:** Because every rep starts dead on the floor, this builds explosive pulling power — keep sets at 5–8 crisp reps.

### Inverted Rows (Remo Invertido)
- **Muscles:** Mid-back, lats, rear delts; biceps and core assist.
- **Equipment:** Bar in a rack at waist height, Smith machine bar, or suspension straps.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie under the bar and grab it slightly wider than shoulders, body straight from head to heels.
  2. Pull your chest to the bar, squeezing your shoulder blades.
  3. Pause briefly at the top.
  4. Lower under control to straight arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Sagging hips.
  - Pulling with the arms only, without retracting the shoulder blades.
- **Tip:** The more horizontal your body, the harder it gets — walk your feet forward to progress toward pull-ups.

### Meadows Rows (Remo Meadows)
- **Muscles:** Lats and upper back (one side at a time); rear delts and biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell in a landmine attachment.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand perpendicular to the landmine bar and hinge at the hips, back flat.
  2. Grab the thick end of the sleeve with an overhand grip (straps help).
  3. Row the bar end up and back toward your hip, elbow flaring out slightly.
  4. Lower to a full stretch; finish the set, then switch sides.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rotating the torso to heave the weight.
  - Cutting the stretch at the bottom.
- **Tip:** Named after bodybuilder John Meadows — the deep unilateral stretch makes 8–12 reps per side plenty.

## Shoulders

### Military Press (Press Militar)
- **Muscles:** Front and side delts; triceps and upper chest assist, core stabilizes.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with feet together or hip-width, bar racked at your collarbone, grip just outside shoulders.
  2. Brace your core and glutes.
  3. Press the bar straight overhead, moving your head slightly back out of the way.
  4. Lock out with the bar over your mid-foot, then lower to the collarbone under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Arching the lower back and leaning away.
  - Pressing the bar forward instead of straight up.
- **Tip:** Strict form, no leg drive — 5–8 reps builds serious overhead strength.

### Lateral Raises (Elevaciones Laterales)
- **Muscles:** Side delts; traps assist slightly.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with a dumbbell in each hand at your sides, slight elbow bend.
  2. Raise both arms out to the sides until they reach shoulder height.
  3. Lead with the elbows, pinkies roughly level with thumbs.
  4. Lower slowly back to your sides.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the weights up with momentum.
  - Shrugging the traps as you lift.
- **Tip:** Go light and strict — 12–20 reps; if you must lean and heave, the dumbbells are too heavy.

### Overhead Press (Press sobre Cabeza)
- **Muscles:** Delts (front and side); triceps and upper chest assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand (or sit) holding the weight at shoulder height, forearms vertical.
  2. Brace your core and press straight overhead.
  3. Finish with arms extended and biceps close to your ears.
  4. Lower under control back to the shoulders.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Flaring the ribs and over-arching the back.
  - Stopping short of full lockout.
- **Tip:** Exhale as you press; 6–10 reps. Seated dumbbell pressing is a good option if standing balance is a limiter.

### Arnold Press (Press Arnold)
- **Muscles:** All three deltoid heads; triceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit holding dumbbells in front of your shoulders, palms facing you (like the top of a curl).
  2. As you press up, rotate your palms outward.
  3. Finish overhead with palms facing forward.
  4. Reverse the rotation on the way down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rushing the rotation instead of blending it smoothly with the press.
  - Excessive lower-back arch.
- **Tip:** The rotation adds range and front-delt work — use lighter weight than a normal press, 8–12 reps.

### Front Raises (Elevaciones Frontales)
- **Muscles:** Front delts; upper chest assists.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells, barbell, or a weight plate.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the weight in front of your thighs, palms down.
  2. With straight (softly bent) arms, raise the weight to shoulder height.
  3. Pause briefly, keeping your torso still.
  4. Lower slowly to the start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rocking the torso backward to swing the weight.
  - Raising above shoulder height with a shrug.
- **Tip:** Front delts get lots of work from pressing already — light weight, 10–15 reps, alternating arms if it helps you stay strict.

### Upright Rows (Remo al Mentón)
- **Muscles:** Side delts and traps; biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, EZ-bar, dumbbells, or cable.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the bar in front of your thighs with a grip about shoulder width.
  2. Pull the bar straight up along your body, leading with your elbows.
  3. Stop when your elbows reach shoulder height.
  4. Lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pulling too high with a very narrow grip (uncomfortable for many shoulders).
  - Jerking the weight with the hips.
- **Tip:** A wider grip and stopping at chest/shoulder height is more comfortable for most people — 10–15 reps.

### Reverse Pec Deck Flyes (Aperturas Inversas en Máquina)
- **Muscles:** Rear delts; mid-back (rhomboids, traps) assists.
- **Equipment:** Pec deck machine set to reverse-fly position.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit facing the machine's pad, chest against it, and grab the handles in front of you.
  2. With slightly bent elbows, sweep your arms back and out.
  3. Squeeze your rear delts and shoulder blades at the back.
  4. Return slowly to the start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Using too much weight and pulling with the arms.
  - Shrugging into the neck.
- **Tip:** Rear delts respond to volume — 12–20 strict reps, pausing one second at the back.

### Push Press (Press de Empuje)
- **Muscles:** Delts and triceps; legs and core drive the movement.
- **Equipment:** Barbell (or dumbbells).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hold the bar at your collarbone in a standing overhead press setup.
  2. Dip by bending your knees a few inches, torso upright.
  3. Drive explosively through your legs and press the bar overhead in one motion.
  4. Lock out, then lower the bar back to the shoulders under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Dipping too deep or too slow, losing the elastic bounce.
  - Letting the torso pitch forward in the dip.
- **Tip:** The leg drive lets you handle more than a strict press — use it for 3–6 powerful reps.

### Bradford Press (Press Bradford)
- **Muscles:** Front and side delts; triceps and upper back assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit or stand with the bar at your collarbone, grip slightly wider than shoulders.
  2. Press the bar just high enough to clear your head.
  3. Move it back and lower to the base of your neck behind the head.
  4. Press back over to the front — that's one rep. Keep the arc low and continuous.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pressing to full lockout each time (the point is constant tension).
  - Going heavy — this is a light, controlled movement.
- **Tip:** Use a light bar, 8–12 slow reps; skip it if you lack the shoulder mobility to pass behind the head comfortably.

### Cable Lateral Raises (Elevaciones Laterales en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Side delts.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a low pulley and single handle.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand side-on to a low pulley, handle in your outside hand (cable runs across your body).
  2. With a slight elbow bend, raise your arm out to the side to shoulder height.
  3. Pause briefly at the top.
  4. Lower slowly; finish the set, then face the other way and switch arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning away from the cable to cheat the weight up.
  - Letting the cable yank the arm down.
- **Tip:** Unlike dumbbells, the cable loads the delt even at the bottom — 12–15 reps per arm feels very different.

### Rear Delt Rows (Remo de Deltoides Posterior)
- **Muscles:** Rear delts and upper back; biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hinge forward with a flat back, holding the weight with a wide overhand grip.
  2. Row with your elbows flared out to the sides, about 90° from your torso.
  3. Pull the weight toward your upper chest/neckline.
  4. Squeeze the rear delts, then lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Tucking the elbows (that turns it into a lat row).
  - Standing up during the pull.
- **Tip:** The elbow-out path is what targets the rear delts — moderate weight, 10–15 reps.

### Handstand Push-Ups (Flexiones en Parada de Manos)
- **Muscles:** Delts and triceps; traps and core stabilize.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight and a wall.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Kick up into a handstand with your heels resting against a wall, hands slightly wider than shoulders.
  2. Brace your whole body and lower your head toward the floor under control.
  3. Lightly touch down (or stop just above the floor).
  4. Press back up to straight arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Over-arching the back with flared ribs.
  - Diving down without control.
- **Tip:** Advanced move — build up with pike push-ups (hips high, feet on a box) before going fully vertical; 3–8 reps per set.

## Biceps

### Barbell Curls (Curl con Barra)
- **Muscles:** Biceps; forearms assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the bar with an underhand, shoulder-width grip, arms extended.
  2. Keeping elbows pinned at your sides, curl the bar to shoulder height.
  3. Squeeze the biceps at the top.
  4. Lower slowly to full extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the torso to heave the bar up.
  - Letting the elbows drift forward at the top.
- **Tip:** The classic mass builder — 8–12 reps; a 3-second lowering phase adds a lot of stimulus.

### Hammer Curls (Curl Martillo)
- **Muscles:** Biceps and brachialis; forearms (brachioradialis) work hard.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing your body (neutral grip).
  2. Curl the weights up without rotating the wrists — thumbs stay up.
  3. Lift to shoulder height, keeping elbows at your sides.
  4. Lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging both dumbbells with body momentum.
  - Turning the wrists into a normal curl.
- **Tip:** Builds arm thickness and grips strength; 8–12 reps, both arms together or alternating.

### Dumbbell Curls (Curl con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Biceps; forearms assist.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing forward (or rotate as you lift).
  2. Curl one or both dumbbells to shoulder height, elbows pinned.
  3. Squeeze at the top with palms fully turned up (supinated).
  4. Lower slowly to straight arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Half reps that never fully straighten the arm.
  - Elbows drifting behind or in front of the body.
- **Tip:** Rotating from neutral to palm-up during the curl maximizes biceps contraction — 8–12 reps.

### Preacher Curls (Curl en Banco Scott)
- **Muscles:** Biceps (especially the lower portion near the elbow).
- **Equipment:** Preacher bench with EZ-bar, barbell, or dumbbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit at the preacher bench with your upper arms flat on the angled pad.
  2. Start with arms nearly extended, holding the bar.
  3. Curl the bar up until your forearms are vertical.
  4. Lower slowly, stopping just short of a harsh full lockout.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lifting the elbows off the pad at the top.
  - Bouncing out of the stretched bottom position.
- **Tip:** The pad eliminates cheating — go lighter than standing curls and control 8–12 reps.

### EZ-Bar Curls (Curl con Barra EZ)
- **Muscles:** Biceps; forearms assist.
- **Equipment:** EZ (zig-zag) curl bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the EZ-bar on the angled grips, palms tilted slightly inward.
  2. Curl the bar to shoulder height with elbows pinned at your sides.
  3. Squeeze the biceps at the top.
  4. Lower under control to full extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning back as the set gets hard.
  - Cutting the bottom range short.
- **Tip:** The angled grip is easier on the wrists than a straight bar — a great default for 8–12 rep curl work.

### Concentration Curls (Curl Concentrado)
- **Muscles:** Biceps (peak emphasis).
- **Equipment:** One dumbbell, bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit on a bench, lean forward, and brace your elbow against the inside of your thigh.
  2. Let the dumbbell hang at full arm extension.
  3. Curl it toward your shoulder without moving the upper arm.
  4. Squeeze hard at the top, lower slowly; switch arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rocking the torso to help the weight up.
  - Letting the elbow slide off the thigh.
- **Tip:** Total isolation — light weight, 10–15 reps per arm with a deliberate squeeze on every rep.

### Incline Dumbbell Curls (Curl Inclinado con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Biceps (long head, stretched position).
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells, incline bench (about 45–60°).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie back on an incline bench with dumbbells hanging straight down, arms fully extended.
  2. Keeping your upper arms vertical, curl both dumbbells up.
  3. Squeeze at the top without letting elbows swing forward.
  4. Lower slowly back to the deep stretch.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the elbows drift forward, killing the stretch.
  - Shortening the bottom range as fatigue builds.
- **Tip:** The stretched start makes light weights feel heavy — 8–12 strict reps.

### Cable Curls (Curl en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Biceps; forearms assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a low pulley and straight/EZ bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand facing the low pulley, grab the bar underhand, elbows at your sides.
  2. Curl the bar to shoulder height against the cable tension.
  3. Squeeze at the top.
  4. Lower slowly, resisting the cable all the way down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Stepping too close and losing tension at the bottom.
  - Leaning back to finish reps.
- **Tip:** Constant cable tension makes this ideal for high-rep finishers — 12–15 reps to a strong pump.

### Spider Curls (Curl Araña)
- **Muscles:** Biceps (short head, contracted position).
- **Equipment:** Incline bench, dumbbells or EZ-bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie chest-down on an incline bench with your arms hanging straight toward the floor.
  2. Curl the weight up while your upper arms stay vertical and still.
  3. Squeeze hard at the top — this position maximizes peak contraction.
  4. Lower to a full hang.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the arms forward and back.
  - Lifting the chest off the bench.
- **Tip:** No way to cheat here — go light and chase the squeeze, 10–15 reps.

### 21s (21s)
- **Muscles:** Biceps.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, EZ-bar, or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Do 7 reps from full extension to halfway up (elbows at 90°).
  2. Then 7 reps from halfway to the top of the curl.
  3. Finish with 7 full-range curls.
  4. That's one set of 21.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rushing the partials with momentum.
  - Starting too heavy — the last 7 full reps are brutal.
- **Tip:** Use roughly half your normal curl weight; 2–3 sets is plenty as a finisher.

### Drag Curls (Curl de Arrastre)
- **Muscles:** Biceps (long head); minimal front-delt involvement.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or EZ-bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the bar with an underhand grip against your thighs.
  2. "Drag" the bar straight up your torso, driving your elbows back behind you.
  3. Keep the bar in contact with (or very close to) your body up to the lower chest.
  4. Lower it back down along the same path.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the bar drift away from the body (that's a normal curl).
  - Shrugging the shoulders to gain height.
- **Tip:** The bar travels less than a regular curl — that's normal. Go lighter, 10–12 reps.

### Cable Hammer Curls (Curl Martillo en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Biceps, brachialis, and forearms.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a rope attachment on the low pulley.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Grab the rope ends with a neutral grip (palms facing each other).
  2. Stand tall with elbows pinned to your sides.
  3. Curl the rope up to shoulder height without rotating your wrists.
  4. Lower slowly against the cable tension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Elbows drifting forward at the top.
  - Using the shoulders to lift as fatigue builds.
- **Tip:** Constant tension plus the neutral grip makes this a great arm-thickness builder — 10–15 reps.

## Triceps

### Skull Crushers (Rompe Cráneos)
- **Muscles:** Triceps (long and lateral heads).
- **Equipment:** EZ-bar, barbell, or dumbbells; flat bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on a bench holding the bar over your chest with a narrow overhand grip.
  2. Keeping upper arms vertical and still, bend only at the elbows.
  3. Lower the bar toward your forehead (or just behind your head).
  4. Extend the elbows to press back to the start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Flaring the elbows out wide.
  - Turning it into a close-grip press by moving the shoulders.
- **Tip:** Lowering slightly behind the head is easier on the elbows — 8–12 controlled reps; go lighter than you think.

### Rope Pushdowns (Extensión con Cuerda)
- **Muscles:** Triceps (lateral head emphasis).
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a high pulley and rope attachment.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Grab the rope with palms facing each other, elbows pinned to your sides.
  2. Push the rope down until your arms are fully extended.
  3. Split the rope ends apart at the bottom and squeeze.
  4. Let your forearms rise back up under control without moving the elbows.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning over the cable and pressing with body weight.
  - Elbows drifting forward and back.
- **Tip:** The split at the bottom is what makes the rope special — 10–15 reps with a 1-second squeeze.

### Triceps Pushdowns (Extensión de Tríceps en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Triceps.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a high pulley and straight or V-bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Grip the bar overhand, elbows bent and pinned to your sides.
  2. Push the bar down until your arms fully extend.
  3. Pause and squeeze the triceps.
  4. Control the bar back up until your forearms pass parallel.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Shrugging and pushing with the shoulders.
  - Letting the elbows flare or drift forward.
- **Tip:** A staple isolation move — 10–15 reps; keep your wrists straight and knuckles pointed down at lockout.

### Close-Grip Bench Press (Press de Banca Cerrado)
- **Muscles:** Triceps; chest and front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, flat bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on the bench and grip the bar at about shoulder width (not narrower).
  2. Unrack and hold the bar over your lower chest.
  3. Lower with elbows tucked close to your torso.
  4. Press back up to full extension, feeling the triceps drive the lockout.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Gripping too narrow and straining the wrists.
  - Flaring the elbows like a regular bench press.
- **Tip:** The best heavy triceps builder — 6–10 reps; keep forearms vertical at the bottom.

### Overhead Dumbbell Extension (Extensión sobre Cabeza con Mancuernas)
- **Muscles:** Triceps (long head, stretched position).
- **Equipment:** One dumbbell (held with both hands) or one per hand.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit or stand holding a dumbbell overhead with both hands under the top plate.
  2. Keep your upper arms vertical, elbows pointing forward.
  3. Lower the dumbbell behind your head until you feel a deep triceps stretch.
  4. Extend back to straight arms overhead.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Flaring the elbows out wide.
  - Arching the lower back to compensate for tight shoulders.
- **Tip:** The long head only gets fully stretched overhead — 10–12 slow reps make this a top triceps-mass move.

### Skullcrushers (Rompe Cráneos)
Same exercise as **Skull Crushers** above — this is an alternate spelling of the same movement in the app. Follow the Skull Crushers instructions: lie on a bench, keep your upper arms vertical, lower the bar toward your forehead or just behind your head by bending only at the elbows, then extend back up. If you log one of the two names, stick with it consistently so your progress history stays in one place.

### Dips
- **Muscles:** Triceps; chest and front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Parallel bars / dip station (bodyweight, optionally weighted).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Support yourself on the bars with arms locked, body upright.
  2. Bend your elbows and lower until your upper arms are about parallel to the floor.
  3. Keep your torso vertical to emphasize the triceps.
  4. Press back up to full lockout.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bouncing at the bottom.
  - Flaring elbows wide.
- **Tip:** Torso upright = triceps; torso leaned forward = chest (see Chest Dips). Build to 8–12 clean bodyweight reps before adding weight.

### Bench Dips (Fondos en Banco)
- **Muscles:** Triceps; front delts and chest assist.
- **Equipment:** Bench or sturdy chair (bodyweight, optional plate on lap).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit on the bench edge, hands next to your hips gripping the edge.
  2. Slide your hips forward off the bench, legs extended (or knees bent to make it easier).
  3. Lower your body by bending the elbows to about 90°.
  4. Press back up to straight arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Dipping too deep and stressing the front of the shoulders.
  - Drifting the hips far away from the bench.
- **Tip:** Bend your knees to regress, elevate your feet or add a plate to progress — 10–15 reps.

### Tate Press (Press Tate)
- **Muscles:** Triceps (elbow-dominant pressing strength).
- **Equipment:** Dumbbells, flat or slight-incline bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on the bench holding dumbbells over your chest, palms facing your feet, elbows flared out.
  2. Bend the elbows and lower the dumbbells inward until the inner plates touch your chest.
  3. The elbows stay high and out to the sides.
  4. Extend the elbows to press the dumbbells back up.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the dumbbells drift toward the face.
  - Going too heavy and losing the unique elbow path.
- **Tip:** A powerlifting favorite for lockout strength — light to moderate dumbbells, 8–12 reps.

### JM Press (Press JM)
- **Muscles:** Triceps; chest and front delts assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or EZ-bar, flat bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set up like a close-grip bench press, bar over your upper chest.
  2. Lower the bar toward your chin/neck area, letting the elbows drop straight down — a hybrid between a skull crusher and a close-grip press.
  3. The forearms fold onto the biceps at the bottom.
  4. Press back up and slightly back to the start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Treating it like a full bench press with a bounce.
  - Grip too narrow, straining the wrists.
- **Tip:** Advanced triceps builder — learn it light for 8–10 reps before loading up.

### Cable Overhead Extension (Extensión sobre Cabeza en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Triceps (long head).
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with rope on a low or mid pulley.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Face away from the machine, holding the rope behind your head, elbows bent and pointing forward.
  2. Step forward into a staggered stance and lean slightly forward.
  3. Extend your arms overhead until fully straight.
  4. Return slowly, letting the triceps stretch behind your head.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Elbows flaring out wide.
  - Arching the lower back instead of bracing.
- **Tip:** Constant cable tension in the stretched position — 10–15 reps; brace your core so the load doesn't pull you backward.

### Single-Arm Cable Pushdowns (Extensión en Polea a Un Brazo)
- **Muscles:** Triceps (one arm at a time).
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a single handle (or no handle, gripping the ball).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand at a high pulley, grab the handle with one hand, elbow pinned to your side.
  2. Push down until the arm is fully extended.
  3. Squeeze the triceps for a second at lockout.
  4. Control the return; finish the set and switch arms.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rotating the torso to press with body weight.
  - Letting the elbow wander off the ribs.
- **Tip:** Great for fixing left-right strength gaps — 10–15 reps per arm; an underhand grip variation hits the triceps slightly differently.

## Forearms

### Wrist Curls (Curl de Muñeca)
- **Muscles:** Wrist flexors (inner forearm).
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells, bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with your forearms resting on your thighs or a bench, palms up, wrists hanging off the edge.
  2. Let the weight roll down toward your fingertips.
  3. Curl it back up by flexing your wrists as high as possible.
  4. Lower slowly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lifting the forearms off the support.
  - Using a jerky, bouncy rhythm.
- **Tip:** Forearms love volume — 15–20 slow reps with light weight and a full stretch.

### Reverse Wrist Curls (Curl de Muñeca Inverso)
- **Muscles:** Wrist extensors (outer forearm).
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells, bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with your forearms on your thighs or a bench, palms down, wrists off the edge.
  2. Let your knuckles drop toward the floor.
  3. Raise the backs of your hands as high as you can.
  4. Lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Going too heavy — extensors are much weaker than flexors.
  - Moving at the elbows instead of the wrists.
- **Tip:** Use noticeably less weight than regular wrist curls — 15–20 reps; balances out all your gripping work.

### Plate Pinches (Pinza de Discos)
- **Muscles:** Grip (thumb and finger pinch strength), forearms.
- **Equipment:** Two smooth weight plates.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Place two plates together, smooth sides out.
  2. Pinch them between your fingers and thumb.
  3. Lift and hold at your side, standing tall.
  4. Hold for time, then set them down under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the plates rest against your leg.
  - Rolling the wrist to make the hold easier.
- **Tip:** Log holds as sets — e.g., 3–4 holds of 20–40 seconds per hand; progress by adding time before adding weight.

### Towel Pull-Ups (Dominadas con Toalla)
- **Muscles:** Grip and forearms; lats and biceps do the pulling.
- **Equipment:** Pull-up bar and one or two sturdy towels.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Drape a towel (or two) over the bar and grab an end in each hand.
  2. Hang with a crushing grip on the towel.
  3. Pull your chin up to hand level as in a normal pull-up.
  4. Lower under control without letting the towel slip.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Using a thin towel that's easy to hold (or too slippery).
  - Kipping — grip usually fails first, so stay strict.
- **Tip:** Brutal grip builder — even 3–5 reps counts; regress with towel dead hangs or towel-assisted holds.

### Reverse Barbell Curls (Curl Inverso con Barra)
- **Muscles:** Brachioradialis and wrist extensors; biceps assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or EZ-bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the bar with an overhand (palms-down) grip, arms extended.
  2. Curl the bar to shoulder height, elbows pinned to your sides.
  3. Keep the wrists straight — don't let them flex back.
  4. Lower slowly to full extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the wrists collapse backward.
  - Swinging the torso as the set gets heavy.
- **Tip:** Expect to use much less weight than normal curls — 10–15 reps builds the top of the forearm fast.

### Wrist Roller (Rodillo de Muñeca)
- **Muscles:** Wrist flexors and extensors, grip endurance.
- **Equipment:** Wrist roller (handle with a rope and hanging weight).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hold the roller with both hands at shoulder height, arms extended (or elbows bent at your sides).
  2. Roll the handle hand-over-hand to wind the rope and raise the weight.
  3. Once the weight reaches the top, reverse and unroll it under control.
  4. Up and down equals one round.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the weight free-fall on the way down.
  - Using the shoulders to swing the roller.
- **Tip:** Light weight burns plenty — 2–3 full up-and-down rounds; alternate rolling direction to hit flexors and extensors.

### Dead Hangs (Colgarse)
- **Muscles:** Grip and forearms; shoulders and lats get a stretch.
- **Equipment:** Pull-up bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Grab the bar with an overhand, shoulder-width grip.
  2. Hang with straight arms, feet off the floor.
  3. Keep shoulders active (slightly pulled down) rather than fully slumped.
  4. Hang for time, then step or drop down softly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging around instead of staying still.
  - Holding your breath.
- **Tip:** Log as timed sets — e.g., 3 hangs of 20–60 seconds; a foundational step toward pull-ups and a great grip test.

### Farmer's Carry (Paseo del Granjero)
- **Muscles:** Grip and forearms; traps, core, and whole body stabilize.
- **Equipment:** Heavy dumbbells, kettlebells, or farmer's handles.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Deadlift a heavy weight in each hand with a flat back.
  2. Walk tall with braced abs and controlled, short steps.
  3. Cover a set distance or time without letting your posture break.
  4. Put the weights down with a flat back.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Shoulders rounding forward as grip fatigues.
  - Rushing with long, sloppy strides.
- **Tip:** This is essentially the same movement as **Farmer's Walk** (listed under Back) — the Forearms listing emphasizes the grip work. Pick whichever you prefer for logging and stay consistent.

### Gripper Training (Entrenamiento con Gripper)
- **Muscles:** Crushing grip (finger flexors), forearms.
- **Equipment:** Hand gripper (spring-loaded).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set the gripper deep in your palm with the fingers wrapped around the handle.
  2. Squeeze the handles fully together.
  3. Hold the closed position for 1–2 seconds.
  4. Open under control and repeat; switch hands each set.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Using a gripper so stiff you can't fully close it.
  - Fast, partial pumps with no control.
- **Tip:** Treat it like a lift — 5–10 hard closes per hand for 2–4 sets, not endless mindless squeezing.

### Finger Curls (Curl de Dedos)
- **Muscles:** Finger flexors and grip; forearms.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit or stand holding the bar with an underhand grip, arms relaxed.
  2. Let the bar roll down your palms to your fingertips, opening the hand slightly.
  3. Curl your fingers to roll the bar back into a full fist.
  4. Squeeze, then repeat slowly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the bar roll too far and drop.
  - Turning it into a wrist curl.
- **Tip:** One of the few direct finger-strength moves — 12–20 slow reps with moderate weight.

## Legs

### Barbell Squats (Sentadillas con Barra)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes; hamstrings, core, and lower back assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, squat rack.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Rest the bar on your upper traps, hands just outside shoulders, and unrack.
  2. Stand with feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out.
  3. Brace your core and squat down by bending knees and hips together, chest up.
  4. Descend until thighs are at least parallel, then drive back up through your whole foot.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Knees caving inward.
  - Heels lifting off the floor.
  - Chest collapsing forward.
- **Tip:** Big breath in at the top, brace, exhale on the way up — 5–8 reps for strength, 8–12 for muscle.

### Leg Press (Prensa de Piernas)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes; hamstrings assist.
- **Equipment:** Leg press machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit in the machine with feet shoulder-width on the platform.
  2. Release the safeties and lower the platform toward your chest under control.
  3. Stop before your lower back rolls off the pad.
  4. Press back up without fully locking the knees.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Going so deep the hips and lower back curl off the seat.
  - Slamming into knee lockout.
- **Tip:** Foot placement changes emphasis — higher on the platform hits glutes/hamstrings more, lower hits quads; 10–15 reps.

### Romanian Deadlifts (Peso Muerto Rumano)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings and glutes; lower back and grip assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand holding the bar at your thighs, feet hip-width, knees slightly bent.
  2. Push your hips straight back, sliding the bar down your legs with a flat back.
  3. Lower until you feel a strong hamstring stretch (usually mid-shin).
  4. Drive your hips forward to stand tall, squeezing the glutes.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rounding the lower back.
  - Bending the knees more and turning it into a squat.
  - Letting the bar drift away from the legs.
- **Tip:** It's a hip hinge, not a squat — 8–12 reps with a slow, stretchy descent.

### Single-Leg Calf Raises (Elevaciones de Pantorrilla a Una Pierna)
- **Muscles:** Calves (gastrocnemius and soleus).
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight, optional dumbbell; a step or plate for range.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand on one foot with the ball of your foot on a step, heel hanging off.
  2. Hold something for balance (or a dumbbell in one hand for load).
  3. Lower your heel below the step for a full stretch.
  4. Rise as high as possible onto your toes, pause, and lower slowly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Tiny bounces with no stretch or full contraction.
  - Bending the knee to cheat the rep.
- **Tip:** One leg at a time doubles the load instantly — 10–15 slow reps per leg with a 2-second pause at the top.

### Barbell Hip Thrusts (Empuje de Cadera)
- **Muscles:** Glutes; hamstrings and quads assist.
- **Equipment:** Barbell (padded), bench.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit on the floor with your upper back against a bench, barbell over your hips.
  2. Plant your feet flat, hip-width, knees bent.
  3. Drive through your heels and lift your hips until your torso is parallel to the floor.
  4. Squeeze the glutes hard at the top with a level chin, then lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hyperextending the lower back at the top.
  - Pushing through the toes instead of the heels.
- **Tip:** The strongest glute exercise in the app — 8–12 reps with a deliberate 1–2 second squeeze at lockout.

### Glute Bridges (Puente de Glúteos)
- **Muscles:** Glutes; hamstrings and core assist.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight (optionally a plate or dumbbell on the hips).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat and hip-width.
  2. Drive through your heels and lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders.
  3. Squeeze the glutes at the top for 1–2 seconds.
  4. Lower slowly back down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Arching the lower back instead of squeezing the glutes.
  - Pushing off the toes.
- **Tip:** Perfect beginner move and warm-up — 12–20 reps; progress to single-leg bridges or barbell hip thrusts.

### Squats (Wide Stance)
- **Muscles:** Glutes, inner thighs (adductors), and quads.
- **Equipment:** Barbell (or bodyweight/goblet style).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set your feet noticeably wider than shoulder-width, toes turned out about 30–45°.
  2. Brace and sit down and back between your legs.
  3. Keep your knees tracking over your toes the whole way down.
  4. Drive up through the whole foot, squeezing the glutes at the top.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Knees collapsing inward out of the bottom.
  - Leaning far forward instead of sitting between the legs.
- **Tip:** The wide stance shifts work from quads to glutes and inner thighs — 8–12 reps; keep the same depth standard as your normal squat.

### Lunges (Zancadas)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes; hamstrings and core stabilize.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand tall, then take a big step forward with one leg.
  2. Lower until both knees reach about 90°, back knee just above the floor.
  3. Keep your torso upright and front knee over the foot.
  4. Push through the front foot to return to standing; alternate legs.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Front knee collapsing inward.
  - Short steps that slam the knee past the toes.
  - Leaning forward on tired reps.
- **Tip:** 8–12 reps per leg; hold dumbbells at your sides to progress without changing form.

### Step-Ups (Subidas al Cajón)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes; calves and core assist.
- **Equipment:** Box or bench (knee height or lower), optional dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Place one whole foot on the box.
  2. Drive through that front heel to lift your body up — don't push off the floor leg.
  3. Stand fully on top of the box.
  4. Step down slowly with control; finish reps on one side, then switch.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Springing off the bottom leg (stealing the work).
  - Using a box so high the back rounds.
- **Tip:** Lower yourself slowly — the descent is half the exercise; 8–12 reps per leg.

### Cable Kickbacks
- **Muscles:** Glutes (one side at a time).
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with a low pulley and ankle strap.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Strap the cable to one ankle, face the machine, and hold it for support.
  2. Hinge slightly forward with a flat back.
  3. Kick the strapped leg straight back and slightly up, squeezing the glute.
  4. Pause, then return under control without letting the stack slam; switch legs after the set.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Arching the lower back to kick higher.
  - Swinging with momentum instead of squeezing.
- **Tip:** Pure glute isolation — light weight, 12–15 slow reps per leg with a 1-second squeeze.

### Bulgarian Split Squats (Sentadillas Búlgaras)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes of the front leg; core stabilizes.
- **Equipment:** Bench (rear foot elevated), bodyweight or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand a big step in front of a bench and place the top of your rear foot on it.
  2. Lower straight down until your front thigh is about parallel to the floor.
  3. Keep your torso mostly upright and front knee tracking over the toes.
  4. Drive through the front heel to stand; finish the set, then switch legs.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Standing too close to the bench (cramped, knee-dominant position).
  - Pushing off the back foot.
- **Tip:** Brutal but effective — 8–12 reps per leg; master bodyweight before holding dumbbells.

### Deadlifts (Peso Muerto)
- **Muscles:** Glutes, hamstrings, and entire back; quads, core, and grip work hard.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with mid-foot under the bar, feet hip-width.
  2. Hinge down and grip the bar just outside your legs; flatten your back and lift your chest.
  3. Push the floor away and stand up, keeping the bar dragging close to your legs.
  4. Lock out tall with squeezed glutes, then lower with a controlled hip hinge.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rounding the lower back off the floor.
  - Jerking the bar with slack arms.
  - Leaning back and hyperextending at the top.
- **Tip:** The biggest full-body lift — keep reps low and crisp (3–6) and treat every rep like the first.

### Leg Extensions (Extensiones de Cuádriceps)
- **Muscles:** Quadriceps (isolation).
- **Equipment:** Leg extension machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with your knees lined up with the machine's pivot and the pad on your lower shins.
  2. Grip the handles and extend your legs until straight.
  3. Squeeze the quads for a second at the top.
  4. Lower slowly to the start without letting the stack drop.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Kicking the weight up with momentum.
  - Lifting the hips off the seat on heavy reps.
- **Tip:** The only true quad isolation in the gym — 10–15 controlled reps with a hard 1-second squeeze.

### Front Squats (Sentadillas Frontales)
- **Muscles:** Quads; glutes, core, and upper back work hard to stay upright.
- **Equipment:** Barbell, squat rack.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Rack the bar across your front delts, elbows high, fingertips or crossed arms holding it in place.
  2. Stand with feet shoulder-width and brace.
  3. Squat down keeping your torso as vertical as possible, elbows up.
  4. Drive out of the bottom without letting the elbows drop.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Elbows dropping, tipping the bar forward.
  - Heels rising in the bottom.
- **Tip:** More quad and less lower-back stress than back squats — 5–8 reps; wrist mobility work helps the rack position.

### Hack Squats (Sentadillas Hack)
- **Muscles:** Quads; glutes assist.
- **Equipment:** Hack squat machine (angled sled).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand on the platform with your back and shoulders against the pads, feet shoulder-width.
  2. Release the handles and lower under control.
  3. Descend until thighs reach at least parallel to the platform.
  4. Drive back up through your whole foot without locking the knees hard.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Placing the feet too low and letting the heels rise.
  - Bouncing at the bottom of the rep.
- **Tip:** The fixed path lets you push quads hard safely — 8–12 reps; feet lower on the platform = more quad.

### Sissy Squats (Sentadillas Sissy)
- **Muscles:** Quads (especially near the knee), hip flexors.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight; something to hold for balance (or a sissy squat bench).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand tall holding a support, feet hip-width.
  2. Rise onto the balls of your feet and lean your torso back.
  3. Bend the knees and push them forward, lowering your body in one straight line from knees to head.
  4. Descend as far as comfortable, then squeeze the quads to return upright.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Breaking at the hips (it's a knee-only movement).
  - Going too deep before building strength.
- **Tip:** An intense quad stretch under load — start with small ranges, 8–12 reps, and hold a support until strong.

### Lying Leg Curls (Curl Femoral Acostado)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings; calves assist slightly.
- **Equipment:** Lying leg curl machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie face down with the pad against your lower calves and knees just off the bench edge.
  2. Grip the handles and keep your hips pressed down.
  3. Curl your heels toward your glutes.
  4. Squeeze at the top, then lower slowly to full extension.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hips popping up to help the weight.
  - Fast, half-range reps.
- **Tip:** Hamstrings respond well to slow negatives — 10–15 reps with a 3-second lowering phase.

### Seated Leg Curls (Curl Femoral Sentado)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings.
- **Equipment:** Seated leg curl machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with your knees at the machine's pivot, back against the pad, and the ankle pad on your lower calves.
  2. Lower the thigh pad snugly over your legs.
  3. Curl your heels down and under the seat as far as possible.
  4. Squeeze, then return slowly to the stretched start.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Scooting the hips forward to shorten the range.
  - Letting the stack yank the legs back up.
- **Tip:** The seated angle stretches the hamstrings more than the lying version — 10–15 reps, full range.

### Good Mornings (Buenos Días)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, and lower back.
- **Equipment:** Barbell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set the bar on your upper traps as if squatting, feet hip-width, knees slightly bent.
  2. Brace, then push your hips back and bow your torso forward with a flat back.
  3. Lower until your torso approaches parallel (or your hamstrings say stop).
  4. Drive the hips forward to stand tall.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rounding the spine under load.
  - Treating it like a squat by bending the knees.
- **Tip:** Start very light — an empty bar is plenty at first; 8–12 slow reps with a strong hamstring stretch.

### Glute-Ham Raises (GHR) (Elevaciones Glúteo-Femoral)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings and glutes; calves and lower back assist.
- **Equipment:** GHD (glute-ham developer) machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set your knees on the pad, feet locked against the footplate, body upright from the knees.
  2. Lower your torso forward toward horizontal by extending at the knees, keeping hips straight.
  3. Go as far as you can control.
  4. Curl yourself back up with the hamstrings to vertical.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Breaking at the hips instead of moving at the knees.
  - Bouncing off the bottom.
- **Tip:** Very demanding — push up with your hands on the pad to assist until you can do 5–8 clean reps.

### Single-Leg Deadlifts (Peso Muerto a Una Pierna)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings and glutes; core and ankle stabilizers work hard.
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell or kettlebell (or bodyweight).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand on one leg, weight in the opposite hand (or both hands).
  2. Hinge at the hip, letting your free leg extend straight back as a counterbalance.
  3. Lower until your torso and rear leg are near parallel to the floor, back flat.
  4. Drive the hip forward to return to standing; switch legs after the set.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rotating the hips open toward the free leg.
  - Rounding the back to reach lower.
- **Tip:** Balance is the limiter at first — start bodyweight, fingertips on a wall if needed; 8–10 reps per leg.

### Nordic Ham Curls (Curl Nórdico)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings (eccentric strength).
- **Equipment:** Ankles anchored — a partner, loaded bar, or pad hooks; cushion for the knees.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Kneel tall with your ankles firmly anchored behind you.
  2. Keeping hips straight, lower your torso toward the floor as slowly as possible using your hamstrings as brakes.
  3. Catch yourself with your hands at the bottom.
  4. Push off lightly and pull with the hamstrings to return to kneeling.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bending at the hips to make it easier.
  - Dropping fast instead of resisting.
- **Tip:** Even 3–5 slow negatives is a real set — famous for building hamstring strength, so progress patiently.

### Kettlebell Swings (Balanceo con Kettlebell)
- **Muscles:** Glutes and hamstrings; core, lats, and grip assist.
- **Equipment:** Kettlebell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width, kettlebell a foot in front of you.
  2. Hinge and hike the bell back between your legs like a snap.
  3. Drive your hips forward explosively — the bell floats to chest height.
  4. Let it swing back down into the next hinge; the arms are just ropes.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Squatting and lifting with the arms instead of snapping the hips.
  - Leaning back at the top.
- **Tip:** It's a hip hinge with power — sets of 10–20 explosive reps; exhale sharply at the top of each swing.

### Standing Calf Raises (Elevaciones de Pantorrilla)
- **Muscles:** Calves (gastrocnemius emphasis).
- **Equipment:** Standing calf machine, Smith machine, or dumbbells on a step.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand with the balls of your feet on the platform, heels hanging off.
  2. Lower your heels for a deep stretch.
  3. Rise as high as possible onto your toes.
  4. Pause at the top, then lower slowly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Quick bouncing with no pause.
  - Bending the knees to push the weight up.
- **Tip:** Calves need full range and time under tension — 10–15 reps with 2-second pauses at both top and bottom.

### Seated Calf Raises (Elevaciones de Pantorrilla Sentado)
- **Muscles:** Calves — the soleus (the deeper muscle, worked best with bent knees).
- **Equipment:** Seated calf raise machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit with the balls of your feet on the platform and the pad on your lower thighs.
  2. Release the safety and lower your heels for a full stretch.
  3. Press up onto your toes as high as possible.
  4. Pause, then lower slowly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Short, springy reps.
  - Loading so heavy the range disappears.
- **Tip:** The bent knee targets the soleus, which loves high reps — 15–20 slow reps.

### Donkey Calf Raises (Elevaciones de Pantorrilla Burro)
- **Muscles:** Calves (gastrocnemius, in a stretched position).
- **Equipment:** Donkey calf machine, or hinged at the hips with weight on the lower back/hips.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Bend forward at the hips (about 90°), forearms on a support, balls of feet on a raised platform.
  2. Let your heels sink into a deep stretch.
  3. Drive up onto your toes as high as you can.
  4. Pause and lower under control.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Rounding the back instead of hinging at the hips.
  - Rushing through the stretch.
- **Tip:** The bent-over position stretches the calves harder than standing raises — 12–15 deliberate reps.

### Calf Press on Leg Machine
- **Muscles:** Calves.
- **Equipment:** Leg press machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit in the leg press with only the balls of your feet on the bottom edge of the platform, legs extended.
  2. Keep a slight knee bend and the safeties within reach.
  3. Let the platform push your toes toward you for a full calf stretch.
  4. Press through the balls of your feet to point the toes, pause, and return slowly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bending and extending the knees to move the weight.
  - Placing the whole foot on the platform, cutting the range.
- **Tip:** Easy to load heavy safely — 12–15 slow reps; never remove the safety catches for this one.

### Jump Rope (Saltar la Cuerda)
- **Muscles:** Calves; quads, shoulders, and forearms assist; great coordination work.
- **Equipment:** Jump rope.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hold the handles at hip height, elbows close to your body.
  2. Spin the rope from the wrists, not the arms.
  3. Jump just 1–2 inches off the floor, landing softly on the balls of your feet.
  4. Keep a steady rhythm with a tall posture.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Jumping too high and landing heavy.
  - Swinging from the shoulders and tiring out fast.
- **Tip:** Log it as sets of continuous jumps (e.g., 3 sets of 50–100). For timed conditioning, use **Jump Rope HIIT** in the Cardio category instead.

### Box Jumps (Saltos al Cajón)
- **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, and calves — explosive power.
- **Equipment:** Sturdy plyo box.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand about a foot from the box, feet hip-width.
  2. Swing your arms back, dip your hips, then explode up onto the box.
  3. Land softly on the whole foot with bent knees, standing fully upright on top.
  4. Step down (don't jump down) and reset.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Landing in a deep squat on a too-high box (that's just tucking the knees).
  - Jumping off the box, which pounds knees and ankles.
- **Tip:** Pick a height you land on softly with a quarter squat — 3–5 sets of 3–5 quality jumps, full rest between sets.

### Goblet Squats (Sentadillas Goblet)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes; core and upper back stabilize.
- **Equipment:** One dumbbell or kettlebell.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hold the weight vertically against your chest with both hands, elbows down.
  2. Stand with feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out.
  3. Squat down between your knees, keeping your chest tall and elbows inside the knees.
  4. Drive up through your whole foot.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the weight pull the shoulders forward.
  - Heels lifting at depth.
- **Tip:** The best way to learn squat mechanics — the front-held weight naturally keeps you upright; 8–15 reps.

### Walking Lunges (Zancadas Caminando)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes; hamstrings and core stabilize.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight or dumbbells.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Step forward into a lunge, lowering the back knee toward the floor.
  2. Both knees bend to roughly 90°, torso upright.
  3. Push through the front foot and step directly into the next lunge with the other leg.
  4. Keep walking forward, alternating legs.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Wobbly, narrow steps (imagine walking on railroad tracks, not a tightrope).
  - Leaning forward as fatigue sets in.
- **Tip:** Count total steps — 10–20 per leg; the continuous walking adds a conditioning element regular lunges don't have.

### Pistol Squats (Sentadillas Pistola)
- **Muscles:** Quads and glutes of the working leg; core and ankle stability throughout.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight (a counterweight held forward can help).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand on one leg with the other leg extended straight in front of you.
  2. Arms forward for balance, squat all the way down on the standing leg.
  3. Keep the free leg off the floor and your heel planted.
  4. Drive back up to standing without touching down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Heel popping up (often an ankle-mobility issue).
  - Collapsing into the bottom instead of controlling it.
- **Tip:** Work up with box pistols (sit back to a bench) or hold a support — even 3–5 per leg is advanced.

### Wall Sits (Sentadilla en Pared)
- **Muscles:** Quads (isometric); glutes and calves assist.
- **Equipment:** A wall.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lean your back flat against a wall and walk your feet out.
  2. Slide down until your thighs are parallel to the floor, knees at 90° over your ankles.
  3. Press your back into the wall, hands off your legs.
  4. Hold the position for time, breathing steadily.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Sitting above parallel to make it easier.
  - Resting the hands on the thighs.
- **Tip:** Log holds as sets — e.g., 3 holds of 30–60 seconds; add time before adding a plate on your lap.

## Abs

### Cable Crunches (Abdominales en Polea)
- **Muscles:** Rectus abdominis (the "six-pack"); obliques assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with rope on a high pulley.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Kneel facing the machine, holding the rope ends beside your head.
  2. Keep your hips still and crunch your ribs down toward your pelvis.
  3. Round your upper back as you flex — the elbows travel toward the knees.
  4. Return slowly until your abs are fully stretched.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pulling with the arms or hinging at the hips.
  - Keeping the spine rigid instead of flexing it.
- **Tip:** One of the few abs moves you can load progressively — 10–15 reps, exhaling hard on every crunch.

### Hanging Leg Raises (Elevaciones de Piernas Colgado)
- **Muscles:** Lower abs and hip flexors; grip and lats stabilize.
- **Equipment:** Pull-up bar.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hang from the bar with straight arms and a still body.
  2. Raise your legs — bent knees (easier) or straight (harder) — to at least hip height.
  3. Curl the pelvis up at the top to make the abs, not just the hips, do the work.
  4. Lower slowly without swinging.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging and using momentum.
  - Stopping short of the pelvic curl.
- **Tip:** Progress from knee raises to straight-leg raises to toes-to-bar; 8–15 controlled reps.

### Plank (Plancha)
- **Muscles:** Entire core (abs, obliques, lower back); shoulders and glutes assist.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set up on your forearms and toes, elbows under shoulders.
  2. Form a straight line from head to heels.
  3. Squeeze your glutes and brace your abs as if about to be poked.
  4. Hold, breathing steadily — don't hold your breath.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hips sagging or piking up.
  - Head dropping or craning up.
- **Tip:** Quality beats duration — three crisp 30–45 second holds beat one saggy 3-minute hold.

### Russian Twists (Giros Rusos)
- **Muscles:** Obliques; rectus abdominis and hip flexors assist.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight, or a plate/dumbbell/medicine ball.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Sit on the floor, knees bent, and lean your torso back about 45°.
  2. Lift your feet (or keep heels down to regress).
  3. Rotate your torso to one side, bringing your hands or the weight beside your hip.
  4. Rotate to the other side — that's one rep per side.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Just swinging the arms while the torso stays still.
  - Rounding the lower back hard.
- **Tip:** Rotate from the ribs, eyes following your hands — 10–20 total twists per set.

### Bicycle Crunches (Abdominales Bicicleta)
- **Muscles:** Abs and obliques.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back, hands lightly behind your head, legs lifted.
  2. Bring one knee in while extending the other leg.
  3. Rotate your torso to bring the opposite elbow toward the bent knee.
  4. Alternate sides in a smooth pedaling rhythm.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Yanking on the neck.
  - Racing through reps with tiny rotations.
- **Tip:** Slow is stronger — 10–15 quality reps per side, touching elbow to knee through torso rotation, not neck pulling.

### Mountain Climbers (Escaladores)
- **Muscles:** Core; shoulders, hip flexors, and legs drive; heart rate climbs fast.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Start in a high plank, hands under shoulders.
  2. Drive one knee toward your chest.
  3. Switch legs quickly, as if running in place horizontally.
  4. Keep hips level and core braced throughout.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hips bouncing high as speed increases.
  - Hands drifting forward of the shoulders.
- **Tip:** Works as core work or conditioning — try 20–30 seconds of fast, clean reps per set.

### Ab Wheel Rollouts (Rueda Abdominal)
- **Muscles:** Entire core, especially anti-extension strength; lats assist.
- **Equipment:** Ab wheel.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Kneel with the wheel under your shoulders, arms straight.
  2. Brace your core and tuck your pelvis slightly.
  3. Roll forward slowly as far as you can without your lower back arching.
  4. Pull back to the start using your abs and lats.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hips sagging and lower back arching at the far point.
  - Bending the elbows to cheat the return.
- **Tip:** Short range done right beats long range done wrong — 6–12 reps; progress distance gradually toward standing rollouts.

### Side Plank (Plancha Lateral)
- **Muscles:** Obliques and deep core; hip and shoulder stabilizers.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your side, forearm on the floor with the elbow under your shoulder.
  2. Stack or stagger your feet and lift your hips.
  3. Form a straight line from head to feet.
  4. Hold, then switch sides.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hips dropping toward the floor.
  - Rolling the chest forward or backward.
- **Tip:** 20–45 seconds per side; regress with knees bent on the floor, progress by raising the top leg.

### Dead Bug (Bicho Muerto)
- **Muscles:** Deep core (anti-extension); coordination between limbs and trunk.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back, arms straight up, knees bent 90° over your hips.
  2. Press your lower back gently into the floor.
  3. Slowly lower one arm overhead and the opposite leg toward the floor.
  4. Return and switch sides, keeping the lower back glued down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lower back arching off the floor as the leg lowers.
  - Rushing and losing the opposite-limb pattern.
- **Tip:** Exhale slowly as the limbs lower — 8–12 controlled reps per side; a perfect low-strain core builder.

### Bird Dog (Perro de Caza)
- **Muscles:** Lower back, glutes, and deep core (anti-rotation).
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Start on all fours, hands under shoulders, knees under hips.
  2. Extend one arm forward and the opposite leg back until both are level with your torso.
  3. Pause 2–3 seconds without letting your hips rotate.
  4. Return and switch to the opposite pair.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Arching the back and lifting the leg too high.
  - Rocking the hips sideways.
- **Tip:** Imagine balancing a cup of water on your lower back — 8–10 slow reps per side.

### Leg Raises (Elevaciones de Piernas)
- **Muscles:** Lower abs and hip flexors.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight (floor or bench).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back, legs straight, hands under your hips or at your sides.
  2. Keeping legs straight (or slightly bent), raise them to vertical.
  3. Lower slowly toward the floor.
  4. Stop just above the floor and raise again without resting.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lower back arching off the floor on the way down.
  - Dropping the legs fast and bouncing.
- **Tip:** If your back arches, shorten the range or bend the knees — 10–15 slow reps.

### V-Ups
- **Muscles:** Full rectus abdominis (upper and lower together); hip flexors assist.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie flat with arms extended overhead and legs straight.
  2. In one motion, lift your torso and legs, folding at the hips.
  3. Reach your hands toward your toes at the top — your body forms a "V".
  4. Lower both halves under control back to flat.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Throwing the arms to build momentum.
  - Bending the knees excessively at the top.
- **Tip:** Tough move — regress with tuck-ups (knees bent) and build to 8–15 crisp V-ups.

### Pallof Press
- **Muscles:** Deep core and obliques (anti-rotation).
- **Equipment:** Cable machine (or resistance band) at chest height.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand side-on to the cable, holding the handle with both hands at your chest.
  2. Step away until there's real tension.
  3. Press the handle straight out in front of you — the cable will try to twist you toward it.
  4. Resist the rotation, hold 2–3 seconds, and return; switch sides after the set.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Letting the torso rotate toward the machine.
  - Standing too close, with no tension to fight.
- **Tip:** The core's job here is to NOT move — 8–12 presses per side with a solid pause.

### Hollow Body Hold
- **Muscles:** Entire anterior core (gymnastics-style bracing).
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back and press your lower back firmly into the floor.
  2. Lift your shoulder blades and legs off the ground, arms extended overhead.
  3. Your body forms a shallow banana shape — lower back stays glued down.
  4. Hold while breathing shallowly and steadily.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lower back popping off the floor (position too advanced).
  - Chin jammed to chest.
- **Tip:** Regress with knees tucked and arms at your sides; build to 20–40 second holds.

### Wood Choppers (Leñador)
- **Muscles:** Obliques and core rotation; shoulders and hips assist.
- **Equipment:** Cable machine (high-to-low or low-to-high) or a dumbbell/medicine ball.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand side-on to a high pulley, holding the handle with both hands above one shoulder.
  2. Pull the handle diagonally across your body to the opposite hip, rotating your torso and pivoting the back foot.
  3. Control the return along the same diagonal.
  4. Finish the set, then face the other way.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Chopping with only the arms while the trunk stays still.
  - Rounding the back at the bottom.
- **Tip:** Power comes from the hips and trunk — 10–12 reps per side, moderate weight.

### Flutter Kicks (Patadas de Tijera)
- **Muscles:** Lower abs and hip flexors.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back, hands under your hips, lower back pressed down.
  2. Lift both legs a few inches off the floor.
  3. Kick your legs up and down alternately in small, quick pulses.
  4. Keep the core braced and legs straight throughout.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Lower back arching as the legs fatigue.
  - Kicks so big the tension disappears.
- **Tip:** Count time, not reps — 20–30 seconds of small fast kicks; raise your legs higher to make it easier.

### Scissor Kicks
- **Muscles:** Lower abs, hip flexors, and inner thighs.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back, hands under your hips, legs raised a few inches off the floor.
  2. Cross one leg over the other horizontally.
  3. Open them apart and cross again with the other leg on top — a side-to-side scissor motion.
  4. Keep the lower back pressed into the floor.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Holding your breath.
  - Legs drifting upward, killing the tension.
- **Tip:** Like flutter kicks but side-to-side — 20–30 seconds per set; combine both for a lower-ab finisher.

### Toe Touches (Toques de Punta)
- **Muscles:** Upper abs; hip flexors assist.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Lie on your back with legs raised straight up toward the ceiling.
  2. Reach your arms toward your toes.
  3. Crunch your shoulder blades off the floor, reaching as high up your legs as possible.
  4. Lower the shoulders back down under control and repeat.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bouncing off the floor with momentum.
  - Pulling the chin to the chest.
- **Tip:** The legs stay still — only the torso moves; 12–20 reps, exhaling on every reach.

## Cardio

All Cardio exercises are time-based in the app: log Duration in minutes (1–120). They have no demo images.

### Treadmill Running (Correr en Cinta)
- **Muscles:** Legs and hips; cardiovascular system overall.
- **Equipment:** Treadmill.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Start walking, then bring the belt up to a comfortable running speed.
  2. Run tall with a slight forward lean, landing lightly under your hips.
  3. Keep a relaxed arm swing and steady breathing.
  4. Finish with 2–3 minutes of walking to cool down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Holding the handrails while running.
  - Overstriding with heavy heel strikes.
- **Tip:** For steady state, run 20–40 minutes at a pace where you could speak short sentences; add a 1% incline to mimic outdoor effort.

### Sprint Intervals (Intervalos de Sprint)
- **Muscles:** Legs, glutes, and core; maximal cardiovascular demand.
- **Equipment:** Treadmill, track, or open space.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Warm up 5–10 minutes with easy jogging and a few build-up runs.
  2. Sprint hard for 15–30 seconds — near-maximal effort.
  3. Walk or jog easy for 1–3 minutes until mostly recovered.
  4. Repeat 6–10 rounds, then cool down.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Sprinting all-out with no warm-up.
  - Cutting recovery so short that later sprints turn into jogs.
- **Tip:** Quality over quantity — a 15–25 minute session including warm-up is plenty; log the total time.

### Swimming (Natación)
- **Muscles:** Full body — back, shoulders, core, and legs; joint-friendly cardio.
- **Equipment:** Pool.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Warm up with a few easy lengths of your most comfortable stroke.
  2. Swim continuous laps or intervals (e.g., one length harder, one easy).
  3. Focus on long strokes, rhythmic breathing, and a steady body position.
  4. Ease off for the final few lengths.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Holding your breath instead of exhaling underwater.
  - Lifting the head high to breathe, sinking the hips.
- **Tip:** New swimmers do better with intervals — swim one length, rest 15–30 seconds, repeat for 15–30 total minutes.

### Stationary Bike (Bicicleta Estática)
- **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, and calves; low-impact cardio.
- **Equipment:** Stationary/exercise bike.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set the saddle so your knee stays slightly bent at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
  2. Pedal at a smooth cadence (about 70–90 rpm).
  3. Adjust resistance so effort feels steady but sustainable.
  4. Keep shoulders relaxed and grip light.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Saddle too low, cramping the knees.
  - Grinding a huge resistance at a very slow cadence.
- **Tip:** 20–45 minutes at conversational effort for steady state, or alternate 1 minute hard / 2 minutes easy for intervals.

### Cycling (Ciclismo)
- **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, hamstrings, and calves; core stabilizes.
- **Equipment:** Bicycle (road, mountain, or commuter) and a helmet.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Check saddle height — slight knee bend at the bottom of the stroke.
  2. Ride at a steady, sustainable pace, shifting gears to keep a smooth cadence.
  3. Stay relaxed through the shoulders and hands.
  4. Follow traffic rules and stay visible.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Mashing big gears at low cadence.
  - Locked elbows and hunched shoulders on long rides.
- **Tip:** 30–60 minutes at a pace where you can hold a conversation builds an excellent aerobic base.

### Elliptical (Elíptica)
- **Muscles:** Legs and glutes; arms and back if you drive the handles; very low impact.
- **Equipment:** Elliptical machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Step on and start pedaling smoothly, hands on the moving handles.
  2. Stand tall — don't lean on the console.
  3. Push and pull the handles to involve the upper body.
  4. Adjust resistance/incline to keep the effort honest.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Slumping over the console and letting momentum do the work.
  - Heels lifting off the pedals.
- **Tip:** Great joint-friendly option — 20–40 minutes steady, or alternate 2 minutes brisk / 1 minute easy.

### Stair Climber (Escaladora)
- **Muscles:** Glutes, quads, and calves; strong cardio stimulus.
- **Equipment:** Stair climber/stepmill machine.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Start at a slow step rate and find your rhythm.
  2. Stand tall with a light fingertip touch on the rails.
  3. Place your whole foot on each step and drive through the heel.
  4. Increase the rate gradually as you warm up.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning heavily on the handrails (it erases most of the work).
  - Tiny toe-taps instead of full steps.
- **Tip:** 15–30 minutes is a solid session — it feels harder than most machines at the same heart rate, so build up gradually.

### Rowing Machine (Máquina de Remo)
- **Muscles:** Legs, back, core, and arms — nearly the whole body.
- **Equipment:** Rowing ergometer.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Strap in and grab the handle, arms straight, shins vertical.
  2. Drive with the legs first, then swing the torso back slightly, then pull the handle to your lower ribs.
  3. Reverse the order on the return: arms out, torso forward, knees bend.
  4. Keep each stroke long and smooth.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pulling with the arms before the legs finish.
  - Hunching the back at the catch.
- **Tip:** Think "legs–body–arms, arms–body–legs" — 15–30 minutes steady, or intervals like 500 m hard / 1 minute easy.

### Ski Erg
- **Muscles:** Lats, triceps, core, and legs — a standing full-body pull.
- **Equipment:** Ski ergometer.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand facing the machine, arms overhead gripping the handles.
  2. Drive the handles down past your hips using lats, abs, and a hip hinge — like a double-pole ski stroke.
  3. Let your knees and hips bend as the arms come down.
  4. Return tall with arms overhead and repeat rhythmically.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pulling with the arms only, no trunk or hip drive.
  - Rounding the back instead of hinging.
- **Tip:** Excellent low-impact conditioning — try 10–20 minutes steady, or 30 seconds hard / 30 seconds easy for 10–15 rounds.

### Assault Bike
- **Muscles:** Full body — legs pedal while arms drive the handles; brutal conditioning tool.
- **Equipment:** Air/assault bike (fan resistance).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Set the saddle so your knee is slightly bent at the bottom.
  2. Pedal and push-pull the handles together — the fan gives more resistance the harder you go.
  3. Push through the whole foot and keep the torso stable.
  4. Settle into a rhythm for steady work or empty the tank for intervals.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Arms just holding on instead of driving.
  - Starting intervals too hard and fading immediately.
- **Tip:** The harder you push, the harder it pushes back — intervals shine here: 20–30 seconds hard, 60–90 seconds easy, 8–12 rounds.

### Battle Ropes (Cuerdas de Batalla)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders, arms, core, and legs; high heart-rate conditioning.
- **Equipment:** Anchored heavy ropes.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Hold a rope end in each hand, feet shoulder-width, knees soft, hips back slightly.
  2. Whip the ropes in alternating waves (or double waves/slams).
  3. Keep the waves flowing all the way to the anchor.
  4. Brace your core and stay low throughout.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Standing tall with stiff legs.
  - Tiny arm flicks that die halfway down the rope.
- **Tip:** Work in bursts — 20–30 seconds of waves, 30–60 seconds rest, repeated for 10–15 minutes total.

### Burpees (Burpees)
- **Muscles:** Full body — chest, legs, core; maximal conditioning effect.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. From standing, squat down and place your hands on the floor.
  2. Jump your feet back to a plank (add a push-up for the full version).
  3. Jump the feet back in toward your hands.
  4. Explode up with a small jump and repeat.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Sagging hips in the plank phase.
  - Landing stiff-legged on the jump.
- **Tip:** Pace them like intervals — e.g., 10 burpees per minute for 5–10 minutes, or 30 seconds on / 30 seconds off.

### Sled Push (Empuje de Trineo)
- **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, calves, and core; huge conditioning demand with no impact.
- **Equipment:** Weight sled on turf or smooth floor.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Grip the poles with arms extended or bent, body in a strong forward lean.
  2. Drive the sled forward with powerful alternating leg strides.
  3. Keep your back flat and core braced — push through the whole foot.
  4. Push for a set distance, rest, and repeat.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Hips too high with short choppy steps.
  - Rounding the back when the sled is heavy.
- **Tip:** No lowering phase means little soreness — 6–10 pushes of 15–30 meters with full recovery, logged as total minutes.

### Sled Pull (Arrastre de Trineo)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, back, and grip; conditioning with zero impact.
- **Equipment:** Weight sled with straps, rope, or harness.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Face the sled holding the straps with tension, or wear the harness facing away.
  2. Walk backward (or forward with the harness) taking strong, deliberate steps.
  3. Keep your chest up and arms as connected hooks.
  4. Pull for distance, rest, and repeat.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Jerky rope tugs instead of continuous leg drive.
  - Leaning so far back you stumble.
- **Tip:** Backward drags are fantastic for the quads and knees-friendly conditioning — pair pushes and pulls in the same session.

### Jump Rope HIIT
- **Muscles:** Calves, shoulders, and core; coordination plus high-intensity conditioning.
- **Equipment:** Jump rope.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Warm up with 2–3 minutes of easy skipping.
  2. Go hard for 30–60 seconds — fast pace, high knees, or double-unders.
  3. Rest or skip slowly for 30–60 seconds.
  4. Repeat rounds for the planned duration.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Jumping high and landing heavily as fatigue builds.
  - Skipping the warm-up rounds.
- **Tip:** 10–20 total minutes is a big session. For casual sets-and-reps skipping, use the **Jump Rope** entry in the Legs category instead.

### Boxing (Boxeo)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders, core, and legs; excellent full-body conditioning.
- **Equipment:** Gloves and a bag or open space (this entry is general boxing training for cardio).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Warm up with light footwork and shadowboxing.
  2. Work rounds — combinations on the bag or in the air — with constant movement.
  3. Keep your guard up and rotate your hips into every punch.
  4. Rest about 1 minute between rounds.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Punching with arms only, no hip rotation.
  - Dropping the hands as you tire.
- **Tip:** Classic structure: 3-minute rounds with 1-minute rest, 4–8 rounds. For specific boxing drills (bag, mitts, footwork), see the Combat category.

### Jumping Jacks (Saltos de Tijera)
- **Muscles:** Calves, shoulders, and hips; simple full-body cardio.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand tall, feet together, arms at your sides.
  2. Jump the feet out wide while raising your arms overhead.
  3. Jump back to the start position in one smooth motion.
  4. Repeat at a steady rhythm, landing softly.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Half-raising the arms.
  - Landing hard on straight knees.
- **Tip:** Great warm-up or interval filler — 30–60 second bursts between exercises, or several minutes at an easy pace.

### High Knees (Rodillas Altas)
- **Muscles:** Hip flexors, quads, calves, and core; sprint-like conditioning in place.
- **Equipment:** Bodyweight.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Run in place, driving each knee up to hip height.
  2. Stay on the balls of your feet with quick ground contacts.
  3. Pump your arms as if sprinting.
  4. Keep your torso tall — don't lean back.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning back to fake knee height.
  - Slow, heavy stomping instead of quick turnover.
- **Tip:** Intervals work best — 20–30 seconds fast, 30–60 seconds easy, for 5–10 rounds or as a warm-up.

## Combat

All Combat exercises are time-based in the app: plan rounds in minutes and log minutes completed (1–120). They have no demo images.

### Shadowboxing (Boxeo de Sombra)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders, core, and legs; footwork and technique under zero impact.
- **Equipment:** None — optionally a mirror or light hand weights.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Take your stance: knees soft, guard up, chin down.
  2. Throw combinations at an imaginary opponent — jab, cross, hooks, uppercuts.
  3. Move constantly: step, pivot, slip, and angle between combinations.
  4. Work 3-minute rounds with 1 minute of rest.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Standing flat-footed and only throwing arms.
  - Dropping the non-punching hand.
- **Tip:** Pick one focus per round (e.g., jab and footwork, then head movement) — 3–6 rounds makes a complete session.

### Heavy Bag Rounds (Rondas de Saco Pesado)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders, arms, core, and hips; power development and conditioning.
- **Equipment:** Heavy bag, gloves, and hand wraps.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Wrap your hands and glove up.
  2. Work the bag in 3-minute rounds: punch in combinations, not single shots.
  3. Move around the bag between combinations, keeping your guard up.
  4. Rest 1 minute between rounds.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Pushing the bag instead of snapping punches.
  - Planting in one spot for the whole round.
- **Tip:** 4–8 rounds is a strong session — sit down on your punches by rotating hips and pivoting the rear foot.

### Speed Bag (Pera de Velocidad)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders and arms (endurance); rhythm, timing, and hand speed.
- **Equipment:** Speed bag on a platform.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand square to the bag, set at about forehead height.
  2. Strike with the side of your fist in small circular motions.
  3. Let the bag bounce three times between hits to start (the classic rhythm).
  4. Alternate hands as you find the timing.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Big looping swings that break the rhythm.
  - Standing too far away.
- **Tip:** Frustrating at first, meditative once it clicks — 3–5 rounds of 2–3 minutes; shoulders burn before technique fails.

### Double-End Bag (Pera de Doble Anclaje)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders and core; sharpens accuracy, timing, and defensive reflexes.
- **Equipment:** Double-end bag (anchored to floor and ceiling), gloves.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Stand in your boxing stance at jab range from the bag.
  2. Snap crisp jabs and short combinations — the bag rebounds fast.
  3. Slip or lean off its return path after each combination.
  4. Work 2–3 minute rounds with 1-minute rests.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Loading up power shots (this bag rewards speed and accuracy).
  - Watching your fists instead of the bag.
- **Tip:** Start with single jabs until you can hit the rebound reliably, then build to jab-cross and add head movement.

### Pad Work (Mitts) (Trabajo de Manoplas)
- **Muscles:** Full body; sharpens combinations, timing, and defense with a partner.
- **Equipment:** Focus mitts and a partner (or coach), gloves.
- **How to do it:**
  1. The pad holder calls combinations ("one-two", "three-two", etc.).
  2. Strike the mitts crisply with proper form, returning hands to guard instantly.
  3. React to the holder's cues — slips, rolls, and counters.
  4. Work 3-minute rounds with 1 minute of rest.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Reaching for the mitts instead of letting them come into range.
  - Sacrificing form for speed.
- **Tip:** The best feedback tool in striking — 3–6 rounds; communicate with your holder about pace and combinations.

### Sparring (Sparring)
- **Muscles:** Full body; live application of technique against a resisting partner.
- **Equipment:** Headgear, mouthguard, 14–16 oz gloves, groin protection; a supervising coach.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Agree on the intensity with your partner — technical/light sparring is the default.
  2. Work 2–3 minute rounds, applying your technique with control.
  3. Prioritize defense, distance, and composure over landing hard shots.
  4. Rest 1 minute between rounds and debrief afterward.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Going too hard — sparring is practice, not a fight.
  - Closing your eyes or turning away under pressure.
- **Tip:** Only spar with proper protective gear and qualified supervision; 3–6 controlled rounds beats one wild brawl for skill growth.

### Kickboxing Rounds (Rondas de Kickboxing)
- **Muscles:** Full body — legs and hips for kicks, shoulders and core for punches.
- **Equipment:** Heavy bag or pads, gloves, and shin guards (for partner work).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Take a kickboxing stance, slightly more square than boxing.
  2. Mix punches and kicks in combinations — e.g., jab-cross-low kick.
  3. Rotate your hips fully and pivot the support foot on every kick.
  4. Work 3-minute rounds with 1 minute of rest.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Kicking with the foot instead of driving through with the shin.
  - Dropping hands while kicking.
- **Tip:** 4–6 rounds; return your kicking leg to stance quickly — balance after the kick matters as much as the kick.

### Kicking Drills (Ejercicios de Patadas)
- **Muscles:** Hips, glutes, quads, and core; flexibility and balance.
- **Equipment:** Heavy bag, kick pads, or open air.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Warm up hips thoroughly with leg swings and light kicks.
  2. Drill one kick at a time — roundhouse, front kick (teep), side kick — in sets of repetitions per leg.
  3. Focus on chambering the knee, rotating the hip, and re-setting to stance.
  4. Alternate legs and kick heights round by round.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Kicking above your current flexibility with rounded posture.
  - No re-chamber — letting the leg just fall after impact.
- **Tip:** Volume builds technique: e.g., 10 kicks per leg per type, 2–3 rounds; slow, perfect reps first, speed later.

### Boxing Footwork Drills (Juego de Pies de Boxeo)
- **Muscles:** Calves, quads, and hips; agility, balance, and ring movement.
- **Equipment:** Open space; optionally an agility ladder, cones, or a line on the floor.
- **How to do it:**
  1. From your stance, drill the basic steps: advance, retreat, lateral steps — the rear or lead foot always follows.
  2. Add pivots off the lead foot to change angles.
  3. Combine movement patterns around cones or a ladder.
  4. Keep your guard up and stance intact the entire time.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Crossing the feet.
  - Bouncing tall with the chin up and hands down.
- **Tip:** Small steps win — 3–4 rounds of 3 minutes; shadowbox while you move so the feet and hands learn to work together.

### Defensive Drills (Slips & Rolls) (Defensa (Esquivas y Giros))
- **Muscles:** Core, legs, and neck stabilizers; head movement and reaction skills.
- **Equipment:** Slip rope/line, a partner with mitts, or solo shadow work.
- **How to do it:**
  1. From your stance, practice slipping: small head movements just outside an imaginary jab, left and right.
  2. Practice rolls (ducks): bend the knees and roll under an imaginary hook, shifting weight side to side.
  3. Move along a slip rope or line, alternating slips and rolls.
  4. Add counters after each defensive move.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Bending at the waist with a stationary head "dodge".
  - Eyes dropping to the floor mid-slip.
- **Tip:** Legs power the defense, not the spine — 3–4 rounds of 2–3 minutes; slow and smooth before fast.

### Grappling Drills (Ejercicios de Grappling)
- **Muscles:** Full body — grip, core, hips, and legs; wrestling and BJJ movement patterns.
- **Equipment:** Mats; a partner for most drills (solo movements work too).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Warm up with solo movements: shrimping (hip escapes), bridges, forward/backward rolls, sprawls.
  2. Drill positional techniques with a partner — takedowns, guard passes, escapes — at cooperative pace.
  3. Repeat each technique in sets on both sides.
  4. Optionally finish with light positional rounds.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Muscling techniques instead of using leverage and hip movement.
  - Skipping the solo-movement fundamentals.
- **Tip:** Log the total mat time — 15–45 minutes; consistency in drilling beats occasional hard rolling for skill development.

### MMA Conditioning Circuit (Circuito de Acondicionamiento MMA)
- **Muscles:** Full body; fight-paced conditioning mixing striking, grappling, and strength elements.
- **Equipment:** Varies — bag, kettlebell or dumbbells, jump rope, open mat space.
- **How to do it:**
  1. Build a circuit of 4–6 stations, e.g., heavy bag combinations, sprawls, kettlebell swings, shadowboxing sprint, burpees.
  2. Work 30–60 seconds per station, moving immediately to the next.
  3. One full circuit = one round (aim for round lengths of 3–5 minutes).
  4. Rest 1 minute between rounds; complete 3–5 rounds.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Sloppy technique once the heart rate spikes.
  - No rest structure — conditioning needs rounds, not chaos.
- **Tip:** Mimic fight structure (5-minute rounds, 1-minute rest) as you advance; keep technique sharp even when exhausted.
