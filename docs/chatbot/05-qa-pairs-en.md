# Gym Tracker — Canned Q&A Pairs (English)

Exact question→answer pairs for the most common user queries. Answers use the app's real button labels. Keep answers this short in chat; point to the other guides for depth.

## Using the app

**Q: How do I add an exercise?**
A: Expand the day, tap **Add Exercise**, then search by name (in English) or filter by muscle group. Set your Target Sets and Reps (or minutes for Cardio/Combat) and tap **Add to Workout**.

**Q: How do I create my own exercise?**
A: Tap **Add Exercise**, switch to the **Custom Exercise** tab, enter a name, sets, and reps, and tap **Add to Workout**. Custom exercises don't have demo images.

**Q: How do I delete an exercise?**
A: Tap the delete (trash) button on the exercise row and confirm. To reorder instead, press and drag the exercise by its drag handle.

**Q: How do I mark an exercise as done?**
A: Tap the **✓** button on the exercise — it turns green and counts toward your weekly progress. Tap **✗** to mark it skipped instead. Tapping again undoes it.

**Q: What does "Effective" mean on an exercise?**
A: It's the number of sets you actually completed, versus your target. For cardio it's the minutes you actually did.

**Q: How do I change a day's muscle groups?**
A: Expand the day, tap the **✏️** icon, select up to 3 muscle groups (or **Rest** for a rest day), then tap **Done**. The label is just a label — you can still add any exercise to any day via the **All** tab.

**Q: How do I make a day a rest day?**
A: Tap the **✏️** icon on that day and choose **Rest**.

**Q: How can I see how to do an exercise?**
A: Tap the **▶** button on the exercise to open "How to do this exercise" — a visual demo of the full movement. 122 of 159 exercises have one.

**Q: Why doesn't my exercise show a demo?**
A: Cardio, Combat, and custom exercises don't have demo images — the app shows "No demonstration available yet". I can still describe the technique if you ask me.

**Q: I searched in Spanish and found nothing.**
A: Exercise names in the app are in English — search "Squats" instead of "Sentadillas". Ask me and I'll tell you the English name of any exercise.

**Q: How do I change the app language?**
A: Tap the **EN/ES** toggle in the header — the whole interface switches instantly.

**Q: How do I see my weekly stats?**
A: Tap the **📊 Weekly Summary** button below the days. It shows totals, sets per muscle group, and the full week detail, with a **Download CSV** option.

**Q: What does "Start New Week" do? Will I lose my plan?**
A: No — it archives the current week and starts a new one **keeping your exercises and weights**, only resetting the completion checkmarks. Past weeks stay viewable in the week navigator.

**Q: What's the difference between "Start New Week" and "Reset Week"/"Reset Day"?**
A: **Start New Week** keeps your customizations and just resets progress for the new week. **Reset Week/Reset Day** restore the original default plan and discard your customizations — use with care.

**Q: How do I see past weeks?**
A: Use the arrows in the week navigator ("Week of …"). Past weeks are read-only; tap **Back to current week** to edit again.

**Q: Why can't I edit anything?**
A: You're probably viewing a past week — they're read-only. Tap **Back to current week**.

**Q: What is the default workout plan?**
A: A 6-day Push/Pull/Legs split: Push (chest/shoulders/triceps) Monday & Thursday, Pull (back/biceps) Tuesday & Friday, Legs Wednesday & Saturday, rest on Sunday. You can change all of it.

**Q: How do cardio exercises work?**
A: Cardio (and Combat) exercises are time-based: you set a target duration in minutes (1–120) and log the minutes you completed. No sets, reps, or weight.

## Training basics

**Q: I can only train 3 days a week — what should I do?**
A: A full-body plan Monday/Wednesday/Friday works great. Ask me for the 3-day plan and I'll give you the exact exercises, or see the Training Setups guide. 4-, 5-, and 6-day options exist too.

**Q: How should I warm up?**
A: 5–10 minutes of light cardio (e.g. Jump Rope or Treadmill — you can add it to your day as a Cardio exercise), a couple of minutes of dynamic moves, then 2–3 lighter warm-up sets of your first big lift.

**Q: How much weight should I start with?**
A: Start light — a weight where you could do a few more reps than planned with clean form. Learn the movement first (use the ▶ demo), then add weight gradually week to week.

**Q: What does 4×8-10 mean?**
A: 4 sets of 8 to 10 repetitions. In the app that's Sets = 4 and Reps = 8-10.

**Q: How long should I rest between sets?**
A: A common guide: 2–3 minutes on heavy compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, presses), 1–2 minutes on smaller isolation exercises.

## Account, sync, and data

**Q: Do I need an account?**
A: No — everything works without one and saves to your device. A free account adds cloud sync so your workouts follow you to any device.

**Q: My workouts don't appear on my other device.**
A: Sign in with the same account on both devices. Data created while signed out lives only on that device until your first sign-in, when it migrates to the cloud automatically.

**Q: I forgot my password.**
A: On the sign-in screen tap **Forgot password?**, enter your email, and tap **Send Reset Link**. The emailed link takes you to a screen to set a new password (minimum 6 characters).

**Q: I didn't get the confirmation email.**
A: Check your spam/junk folder and confirm the address is correct. You can retry from the same screen.

**Q: How do I sign out?**
A: Open the profile menu in the header and tap **Sign Out**.

**Q: What is the trainer code at sign-up?**
A: It's optional — only for people whose personal trainer gave them a code. Signing up with it links your account to that trainer so they can manage your plan. Leave it empty if you train on your own.

**Q: I'm a trainer — how do I get my clients connected?**
A: Trainer accounts are created via an admin-issued invitation. As a trainer, your panel (🏋️ button) shows your invite code and link — clients who sign up through it are assigned to you automatically.

**Q: Is my data private?**
A: Yes. Cloud data is stored per-account and protected server-side; only you — and your trainer or an admin, if applicable — can access your plan.

**Q: Is the app free?**
A: Yes, the app is currently free to use.

**Q: How do I install the app on my phone?**
A: Open it in your mobile browser and use "Add to Home Screen" (iOS Safari: Share → Add to Home Screen; Android Chrome: menu → Install app). It then works like a native app, even offline.

**Q: Does it work offline?**
A: Yes — it's an installable web app that works offline, saving locally and syncing when you're back online and signed in.

**Q: Can I use kilograms?**
A: The weight field is labeled lbs but accepts any number — you can consistently enter kg if you prefer. The app doesn't convert units. A unit toggle isn't available yet.

**Q: I found a bug / I have a feature idea.**
A: Use the **Share Your Feedback** button at the bottom of the main screen — it goes straight to the developer.

## Health boundary

**Q: My knee/shoulder/back hurts when I do X.**
A: That's something a doctor or physical therapist should look at — I can help with the app and general technique, but I can't give medical advice. It's safest to pause that exercise until a professional checks it.

**Q: Can you give me a diet / meal plan?**
A: I can't give nutrition prescriptions. As a general pattern, balanced meals with protein spread across the day serve most people — for a personalized plan, a registered dietitian or doctor is the right resource.

**Q: I feel dizzy / chest pain when training.**
A: Please stop exercising now and seek medical attention promptly. I can't evaluate symptoms — a doctor should.
